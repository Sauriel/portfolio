# Date 1 - The Biology Student

Emilyth Benoît stood in the middle of the bathroom, her brow slightly furrowed, trying to tame her little horns with a hair clip. But every time the clip covered them on one side, the horn on the other side slipped back out.

"That just looks silly..." she muttered, pursing her lips. The hair clip didn't answer. But if it had, it probably would have said, "Give up."

One glance at the clock was enough to set her heart racing. Her first date in three months. And she was already a nervous wreck. Just yesterday, her sister, Vivienne, had encouraged her: "This time you stay until dessert, or I'll start setting you up with anyone who has even a faint pulse." Maybe that had been a threat? Emilyth wasn't entirely sure. She had only smiled and nodded—and swore to herself that *this time* she would actually make it through.

Only... that was easier said than done. After all, she was of age now. And thus, finally a full-fledged succubus. The last of her kind. If you ignored her sister. And her mother. And her father, if you counted an incubus. In short, she was the last in a brief lineage of extraordinary beings who had inspired human imagination for millennia while stealing their life force. Well, actually, they drank it. And not all of it. A bit like an energy drink. Except that, somehow, you usually had to be naked and sweaty to do it. The thought of it made Emilyth's facial expression perform a strange dance. She had to pull herself together. She would manage this time. After all, a succubus could drive people wild with just a look. In theory, anyway. In practice, Emilyth mostly just made waiters worry that she was about to faint.

Taking a deep breath, Emilyth slipped into her shoes, threw open the door, and ran. It was one of those runs where you wanted to be fast, but at the same time tried not to look desperate. The result was a strange sort of hopping, her skirt fluttering precariously in the wind while her tail lashed back and forth like a nervous metronome needle.

The bell above the door of "Café Amarante" chimed as she stumbled in—and at that exact moment, she actually tripped over the small step she missed *every single time*.

"Are you okay?" someone behind the counter asked, but Emilyth only raised a hand without looking, hoping her frantic breathing didn't sound too much like a panic-induced asthma attack.

No time for the barista. No time for hot chocolate. No time to gather herself. Her date was waiting. She let her gaze sweep across the room and spotted him immediately: a young man at the window table, leaning back casually, sunbeams tracing the contours of his face as he waited for her with a smile. *Okay. Just walk over. Sit down. Breathe. Breathing is sexy, right?*

Emilyth rushed over to him and sat down. "HiI'mEmilysorryI'mlateIhadtodomyhairandthenIdidn'tknowwhattowearandhowareyou?" she blurted out in a single breath, not even attempting to pause for air. She looked at her date expectantly. And met a deeply bewildered face.

Jonas—she had asked him for his name at the supermarket—had bright green eyes that reminded her of candied apples. His dark blonde hair looked as if he had just walked out of a storm, and his three-day stubble gave him a slightly rugged look. She could already picture herself in his arms while they sailed around the world together. In short, he was totally her type.

"Um... what?" Jonas finally answered, pulling Emilyth out of her future planning. "Hi, I'm Emily. Sorry I'm late. I had to do my hair and then I didn't know what to wear. And how are you?" she asked again. This time, way too slowly. Jonas smiled as if he had already made up his mind about her. *Perfect start,* she thought. *Maybe today I'll wait until after the appetizer to run away.*

"You look stunning," Jonas assured her, and her heart fluttered so hard she could almost hear it. Hear it? Her wings! Her wings were fluttering with excitement. Emilyth tried desperately to calm down. Not that a human could see her wings. But she still felt self-conscious.

"So, tell me about yourself," Jonas began, crossing his arms casually on the table. *Okay, that's easy. Just be honest.* "I... come from a family of... uh... very... traditional... uh... caterers." Technically, it was true. Sort of. "Oh, cool. Like a restaurant or something?" – "Uh... or something," Emily confirmed.

"I'm still studying. Biology," Jonas shared simply. "And I work at the zoo." *Animal lover.* That was a huge plus. She could practically picture him in safari gear, entering the lion enclosure to tend to a poor, injured lion.

"Ah, sorry, I already ordered. Wasn't sure when you'd get here," Jonas apologized as the barista, Björn, arrived with a small tray and set a steaming cup smelling of cappuccino in front of him. Emily was served a large mug of hot chocolate with a four-leaf clover patterned in cocoa on the foam. "Thanks," she said, turning back to Jonas. "I... um... come here quite often. He knows what I drink," she tried to explain. "And what do you do at the zoo?" she asked, taking a sip of her cocoa.

"I sell cotton candy," he answered, as if it were the most normal thing in the world. "I actually wanted to do something with animal care, but they didn't need anyone right now—and hey, free cotton candy." He grinned.

Jonas blew gently on his cappuccino, while Emily sipped her hot chocolate as if her life depended on it. *Not too much at once. Otherwise, he'll see how shaky my hands are. Why is chocolate always liquid when you're nervous, anyway?*

"Anyway, it's never boring at the zoo," Jonas began, resting his elbows casually on the table. "The other day, a little monkey escaped from its enclosure and stole a visitor's ice cream. The guy looked as if his entire life had just collapsed."

Emily laughed softly, way too high-pitched, and nearly choked. "Oh! That... that's... um... funny. I mean, not for the man. For the monkey. But somehow for you too. So... um..." She buried her lips back in her mug. *Great. I sound like a parrot on caffeine.*

Jonas grinned, as if he had hoped for exactly that kind of reaction. "You should have seen how he held the ice cream. With both hands. So proud. Like a little king."

Emily pictured it and giggled automatically. Her tail twitched under the table, making her cross her legs frantically as if she could hide it that way. *Pull yourself together! Tail-wagging is not sexy. Except maybe for dogs.*

"And you?" Jonas asked suddenly. "Do you have a secret superpower too? I mean, I can negotiate with primates, at least."

Emily blinked. Several times. *Superpower? Oh no. My superpower is wanting to spontaneously sink into the floor.*  
"Uh... I'm pretty good at... um... listening to people?" she tried. "It doesn't sound spectacular, but... then again, I don't meet many monkeys."

Jonas laughed, a deep, warm laugh that hit Emily right in the stomach. *Okay. Wait. Stop. I like that too much. This is dangerous.*

"Well," he said, leaning a little closer to her, "I'd say listening is a pretty great superpower. Especially when you look so cute doing it."

Emily froze, as if someone had pressed the pause button. *Cute?! Did he... did he just really...?! Oh no, now I'm blushing. Oh no, now he sees that I'm blushing. Oh no, now he definitely thinks I'm blushing because I like him... which is true, but...*

"C-cute?" she stammered, giggling nervously. "I mean... uh... I think that was the cocoa. The cocoa is sweet. Not me. Well... I am too, but... no! I mean, not the way you think!"  
Her wings twitched under her sweater, and she frantically pulled it tighter around her.

Jonas watched her, his eyes sparkling, seemingly highly amused. "Okay," he said slowly, "then let's agree: the cocoa is sweet. And you are... at least just as sweet."

Emily sank a bit lower into her chair, undecided whether she should die of embarrassment or just faint on the spot. *Why, universe, why?*

"You know," Jonas continued, his voice taking on a playful undertone that made Emily's heart beat even faster, "I think this is my new favorite spot. Right by the window, good coffee... and the best company you could ask for."

Emily gasped for air like a fish out of water. "Th-the... best... company?" – "Of course." He leaned back and studied her with a smile that hovered somewhere between charming and dangerous. "I mean, the monkey with the ice cream was funny... but I think you even top that."

*Oh no. This is flirting. He is flirting. Officially.*

Emily hastily grabbed her mug and took a way too large gulp of cocoa, just to do something, while Jonas's gaze hit her like a spotlight.

Jonas grinned as Emily practically clung to the mug at her lips. "You know, at the zoo we always say: if you look a llama too deeply in the eyes, you fall in love. Because of the eyelashes." He paused meaningfully. "I think you'd better not look in the mirror. Otherwise, you might fall for yourself."

Emily was right in the middle of swallowing. The cocoa hit her like a blow. She sputtered, choked, and the next moment, the chocolate sprayed back out through her nose.

"Oh no, oh no, oh no...!" she gasped, her eyes watering as she fumbled for napkins. Jonas held his hand over his mouth, but his grin was impossible to hide. "Holy chocolate," he murmured, "that was... even sweeter."

Emily wanted to sink into the floor. "I... I... that wasn't...! It was the cocoa! I swear!"

"Of course," Jonas replied, handing her a napkin with a deadpan face. "The cocoa attacked you. Mean of it. Should I sue it on your behalf?"

Emily sniffled and pressed the napkin against her nose as if tending to a nosebleed. "This is soo embarrassing..." she mumbled behind the paper.

Jonas took a sip of his cappuccino, totally relaxed, and tilted his head slightly. "Hey, don't worry about it. You're the first person who's managed to impress me with hot chocolate. Usually, it takes at least some fire-spitting to do that."

"Impress?!" Emily lowered the napkin. Her eyes were watery from coughing, her cheeks bright red. "That wasn't... uh... not..."

"Oh, yes," Jonas interrupted, grinning. "Definitely. I mean, who else can claim they get to enjoy chocolate twice? Once going in, once coming out."

Emily gasped in mock outrage before quickly raising the napkin again to hide her red face. *I. Am going to die. Right now.*

Jonas leaned a bit closer to her, lowering his voice, and said casually: "You know... if it gets too embarrassing for you here, we could just head back to my place. No barista there, but definitely no one who'll laugh at cocoa accidents."

Emily froze. Her fingers clenched around the napkin. Her heart stumbled like a nervous marathon runner. *To his place? After the date? Just like that?! Oh no. Oh no. That means... that definitely means...*

Emily stood up so abruptly that her chair almost tipped over. "I... um... restroom!" she squeaked, stumbling past the tables with half the cocoa still on her face, looking as though she were searching for an emergency exit.

The door to the ladies' room clicked shut behind her, and she leaned heavily against the sink. In the mirror, she saw her red, watery-eyed face, her messy hair, a chocolate stain on her sweater. Her small horns peeked out from between her strands of hair, as if they had decided to highlight her humiliation.

"I can't do this... I can't do this!" she whispered, frantically splashing water onto her face. "Go to his place? Already? That's... that's too fast. Too soon!"

She spoke to her reflection as if it could offer advice. "You go back out there, smile charmingly, and say: Sure, Jonas, let's go to your place. Totally casual. Totally... oh no..."

Her wings twitched out uncontrollably, their tips almost brushing the toilet stall door. Emily frantically pressed them back under her sweater. *If he's inviting me over... does he think...?* Her heart raced. *Oh God. Of course he thinks that! Men always think that! But I can't do this. I can't—*

She gasped for air, looking around feverishly. A tiny window stood open, far too small for a normal escape. For Emily, it was perfect.

"Okay... okay... Plan B," she muttered, climbing clumsily onto the windowsill. "Next time I'll hold out. Next time... really."

With one last look at her stained reflection, she pulled herself through—and vanished, half-elegantly, half-disastrously, into the night.

The cool night air hit her the moment she landed on the sidewalk outside. Emily scrambled up, vainly dusting off her skirt, and took off running as if pursued by a thousand invisible eyes. Her heart beat even faster than it had during the date, and she didn't dare look back even once.

"Why... do I always do this...?!" she gasped, out of breath. Her tail lashed out of control to the rhythm of her steps, while her wings twitched frantically under her sweater. "I could have just... smiled. Or at least said goodbye. Instead of... a ninja escape through the window."

She stopped outside her apartment, catching her breath, and leaned heavily against the door. She rummaged through her bag, found her key—only after three minutes of frantic searching, of course—and unlocked the door.

Inside, it was quiet. Only the hum of the refrigerator. Emily let herself slide down the wall, burying her face in her hands. "Disaster... a total date disaster..."

And yet—as she sat there, shivering, with cocoa stains on her sweater and damp hair from the bathroom sink—a small, barely containable smile crept onto her lips.

Jonas's laugh echoed in her mind. That warm, deep laugh that had tickled her stomach. And the look in his eyes when he told her she was cute.

Emily pulled her knees to her chest and buried her face in them. "Damn it," she whispered. "I like him."
