# Date 5 - The Professor

Her literature professor's office smelled of coffee, paper, and a bit of dust. Stacks of books piled against the walls like a fortress. And right in the middle sat he—Professor Moreau: grey temples, a neatly trimmed three-day stubble, and that quiet authority that simultaneously calmed and intimidated Emily. He reminded her a little of her father when she caught him reading in his study.

Emily kneaded her lucky charm—today, a little plush shark—in her pocket as if her life depended on it. *Okay, Emily. Just listen. Nod. Maybe even smile. It's just about your paper. Nothing bad.*

"Miss Benoît," he began, flipping through a few pages, "I have read your latest paper. And although it is... unconventional, I must say, it surprised me. Pleasantly surprised me."

Emily's eyes went wide. "Surprised?! I mean... uh... in a positive way? Like... uh... in a positive way? Not like a 'Oh God, what a disaster' kind of surprise? Because... that would also be... well... typical of me."

A faint smile played on his lips. "In a positive way, yes. You have a way of making language come alive that is rarely found."

Emily felt her cheeks grow hot. *He... he praised me. Oh no, why does this feel like he just told me I look pretty?!* "Th-thank you! You also have... very... beautiful handwriting!" she blurted out.

Silence. The professor blinked, then laughed softly—deep and warm. Emily wanted to sink into the floor. *BEAUTIFUL HANDWRITING?! Who on earth says that?!*

He set the pages aside, folded his hands, and looked directly at her. Too directly. Emily remembered Rule Number 2 of her dating code: **No eye contact longer than 5 seconds.** But now she was already at... oh no, at least 7! Maybe 10!

His gaze was warm, but also intense. "Your eyes, Miss Benoît... they remind me of old poems. Full of depth, but also full of secrets. Dangerously beautiful."

Emily gasped quietly. *Dangerously?! Beautiful?! Did he really just say that?* "Your... um... your voice is also... very pleasant," she stammered, instantly cursing herself. "I mean... almost too pleasant! Well... not that you are too pleasant! But... you know..."

A smile flickered across his face, as if he had just heard the most entertaining poem of his life. "You have a charming way of giving compliments, Miss Benoît."

Emily turned bright red. "Compliments?! Oh, no, that wasn't a compliment at all! Well... maybe it was. But I didn't mean it that way! I mean... yes, I did... oh God..." She bit her lip, wanting to look away, but she couldn't. His eyes held her fast, like a magnet. *This isn't normal. This is succubus magic. Or... worse... genuine attraction.*

Professor Moreau leaned back in his chair, folding his hands, his gaze fixed on Emily. It wasn't the strict, academic look she had expected. It was something else. Playful, almost curious, as if he had just discovered a character from a novel who was more exciting than any syllabus.

"You do know," he began slowly, "that you always blush when I praise you? It makes you even more interesting."

Emily gasped. "I-I'm not blushing at all! I'm just... uh... hot! I mean, the office is warm. And the lamp! And... your eyes are... warm too!" Her hand slapped over her mouth in panic. *Oh no, did I just say that OUT LOUD?!*

The professor raised an eyebrow, his smile widening. "My eyes? Warm, you say? I don't hear that very often."

Emily flailed her arms, as if she could wave the words back. "I didn't mean... well... not that they're warm like... cozy! But... well... cozy, yes, but not in a way that I... oh no..." She buried her face halfway in her hands. *Why do I always sound like a broken teapot?!*

He laughed softly, that deep, pleasant voice vibrating through the room. "You are refreshingly honest, Emily. Most students would be incredibly tense. But you... you speak what you feel."

Emily wanted to protest, but instead, she scored an even bigger self-goal: "And you... you speak as if you're... flirting."

Silence.

She gasped, slapping her hand against her forehead. "Oh no! That wasn't... I didn't want... I mean... maybe I did... but not with you! I mean... of course, one could... but... I mean..."

Professor Moreau leaned forward slightly, his voice deep and velvety: "Perhaps I am."

Emily felt her heart do a flip. Her wings twitched under her sweater, her tail lashing nervously against the chair. *He's flirting. Officially. With me. My professor is flirting with me. Help!*

Professor Moreau stood up slowly from his chair, as if to end the conversation. But instead of heading straight for the door, he took a step closer. Only one. But that single step made Emily's heart hammer like a jackhammer.

He reached for the stack of books at the edge of the desk, casually arranging them—and his hand almost brushed hers. Not quite. Just close enough for Emily to feel the warmth.

"You really are extraordinary, Emily," he said softly. "Your words... and your eyes... they make you forget that you only wanted to discuss grades."

Emily gasped. Her mouth was dry. *Oh no. Oh no. That sounds... that sounds like an invitation to a kiss! What if he...*

Her fingers twitched. For a split second, she really... really... wanted to touch his hand. Or worse: kiss him.

"I... I..." Her voice cracked. "I have... uh... another essay in the oven!"

Silence.

The professor blinked. "An... essay in the oven?"

"Yes! I mean... a real one! Very hot! If I don't run right now, then... uh... all of science will burn!" Emily grabbed her bag, promptly knocking over one of the stacks of books, which landed on the floor with a crash. She tried to pick up the books at first, but then stopped and kept running, one of the books still in her hand. "Oh no! I'm sorry! I... I'll bring it back later! Or never! But THANK YOU!"

And before he could reply, Emily had already vanished through the door, half-stumbling, half-flying, her tail lashing frantically behind her like an excited exclamation mark. *Maybe I should change my major. Biology sounds good. No! Not biology! Medicine?*
