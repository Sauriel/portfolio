# Date 7 - The Muse

Emily Benoît stood at the edge of the festival grounds, feeling as if she had accidentally landed in an anthill. Except these ants were blinking colorfully, screaming, laughing, spilling drinks, and trying to be everywhere at once. To her left, someone was dancing in a unicorn costume; to her right, a guy with glitter in his beard was trying to take a selfie while juggling. And rising above everything was the bass, so deep that Emily's heart skipped a beat every single time.

*Okay. Stay calm. Breathe.* She inhaled and regretted it instantly. Her head went light from all the endorphins in the air, and her tongue tingled from the sweet dopamine and sharp noradrenaline. *Ha, I know things. That documentary about the brain paid off. Now I finally know why I want to kiss everyone here...*

She smoothed down her glittery top, brushing her bare midriff. Her outfit was a compromise between her and Vivienne. A crop top and fishnets instead of a sweater and leggings, paired with a skirt and sneakers instead of hot pants and boots. "In your sweater, the most you'll do is sweat a little seductively," her sister had remarked. That had finally convinced Emily. A succubus's sweat would only have attracted more people. She looked around. Midriff-baring tops, shorts, and colorful tights were everywhere. She didn't actually stand out at all. Still, she clutched her lucky charm—today, a little plush star—tightly in her bag.

"You can do this," she whispered to the star. "We'll survive this. Or we'll die sparkling. Either is fine."

A couple giggled as they walked past her, wearing matching shirts that read **"Bass"** and **"Drop"**. Emily blushed instantly. *Matching shirts. That means they're together. Oh God. This must be one of those couples' places. I should have brought a shirt. Maybe one that says 'Flight Risk.'*

The air smelled of roasted almonds, deep-fried cheese, and that sweet, spicy smoke wafting over from some food truck. Emily's stomach growled. Loudly. She quickly pressed her hand against it. "Not now! We're here on a date, not to eat our way through every food truck," she scolded it quietly. Her stomach answered with an even louder growl.

And then she saw her.

In the middle of the bustle stood a woman who looked as if the music itself had created her. Curly black hair, a dress fluttering in the wind as if dancing to an invisible rhythm, and eyes—oh, those eyes!—that sparkled from afar, as if she had swallowed little stage lights.

Euterpe. Or, as her friends called her: Tepi.

Emily stopped dead in her tracks. Her heart skipped three beats, only to resume at double speed. *That's her. Dancing lost in thought, as if she were one of the muses of music. And she looks... she looks so... so...* Her thoughts stumbled, just like she often did. *Oh God, why am I here? I'm not cool enough for her. I'm not even cool enough for a festival wristband.*

Tepi turned toward her as if she had heard Emily's thoughts. Her smile was warm, inviting, and somehow in sync with the music. She waved as if they had known each other forever. "Emily!"

Emily raised her hand. Or at least tried to. What came out was a half-hearted flail that looked like she was trying to shoo away a flying mosquito. "H-hello!" she called back, her voice almost swallowed by the bass.

She stumbled through the crowd, every step a minor adventure: someone bumped into her with a plastic beer cup, she dodged a cloud of soap bubbles, almost stepped on a unicorn tail, and barely saved herself from a skateboard someone was riding right through the throng. Her tail lashed nervously behind her—thankfully invisible to the humans. *Naturally. I'm the only creature here who is thrown off balance by bass and beer showers at the same time.*

Finally, she stood in front of Tepi. "Hi," she gasped, trying to look casual. Instead, she panted as if she had just finished a marathon. *Stay cool. Totally cool. Breathing is cool. Maybe dying is too.*

"You made it," Tepi said, her voice like a melody slicing through the noise without having to be loud. She tilted her head slightly. "And you look... cute."

Emily gasped quietly. "C-cute? Me? Well... uh... no! I mean, yes! I mean... glitter-cute! Not... not... well..." Her hands fluttered helplessly through the air as if trying to recapture the words.

Tepi laughed softly, and the laugh was like a chord anchoring itself instantly in Emily's chest. "Glitter-cute is underrated," she said, casually touching a bracelet jingling on her wrist. It sounded like little bells ringing in perfect time with the music.

Emily wanted to say something. Something clever. Or at least not catastrophic. Instead, what came out was: "I... I'm hungry."

Silence. At least in Emily's head. On stage, the DJ was jumping around, shouting "Let me hear youuuu!", but all Emily heard was her own impending doom.

Tepi only smiled wider. "Then let's get something to eat. There's a food truck here with the best sweet potato fries in the world."

"Fries... yes... perfect." Emily nodded way too hard, nearly sending her hair clip flying. *Sweet potato fries. Healthy! That sounds sensible. That's practically salad. Salad in stick form. I am such an adult woman.*

They set off, and Emily noticed her knees felt like jelly. She tried to walk to the beat of the music, but every time she thought she had the rhythm, she tripped over her own feet.

"Are you okay?" Tepi asked, her voice concerned but still melodic.

"Yes! Totally! I'm just dancing. I mean... walk-dancing. A new concept." Emily grinned lopsidedly. *Great. Now she thinks I'm a walking disaster with a built-in metronome.*

Tepi laughed again, and Emily felt something inside her blossom and collapse at the same time. *Maybe... maybe it's not so bad that I'm like this. Maybe... she actually likes it?*

But then her gaze lingered too long on Tepi's eyes. Warm, sparkling, deeper than any melody. A shiver ran down her spine. *Oh no. Rule Two. No direct eye contact longer than five seconds. Succubus effect! Hypnosis smile! Disaster incoming!*

Emily blinked in a panic, looked away, and stared at the ground instead—only to almost step on a painted rainbow.

"Beautiful, isn't it?" Tepi asked, as if she hadn't noticed a thing. "A little color in the chaos."

"Yes," Emily breathed, "beautiful..." And this time, she didn't mean the rainbow.

The food truck smelled of deep-fried sweet potatoes, spicy sauce, and that magical something that compelled people to order even if they weren't actually hungry. Emily stood before it as if before a temple. A very greasy, but tempting, temple.

"So," she began to whisper, "this is the moment where you look cool. Just order, like everyone else. Don't stammer, don't..."

"Next, please!" the vendor called out.

Emily gasped. "M-me!" Her hand shot up like a student raising her hand because she knows the right answer.

The vendor eyed her. "What'll it be?"

Emily stared at the board. *Sweet potato fries. You know this. Tepi said it. Just say fries. Anyone can say fries. F-r-i-e-s. Simple letters. Only five.*

"Uh... the... well... the sticks... with... um... potato? But sweet... I mean, fries! Sweet! Sweet potato fries! Please! Thank you!"

Silence. The vendor raised an eyebrow. Someone giggled next to her. Emily wanted to sink into the ground.

"One portion of sweet potato fries," he repeated dryly, scribbling on a slip of paper. "And sauce?"

Emily's eyes went wide. *Sauce?! Oh no, a decision! Sauce is... final!*

"I... uh... the... red? Or white? Maybe... both?" – "We have mayo, ketchup, and Andalouse," the vendor said.

"Ah! Yes! Of course! Mayo! ...or ketchup... uh... both?"

"Mayo and ketchup," he sighed, pushing the ticket along.

Behind her, Tepi giggled softly. And that giggle wasn't mocking; it was like a little rhythm that made Emily's heart beat faster. "You're cute when you're overwhelmed."

Emily gasped. "O-overwhelmed? Me? No! This is just... uh... my culinary strategy!"

Tepi grinned, ordered something for herself too—loaded fries with cheese and pulled chicken that looked like they were sponsored by Instagram—and led Emily to one of the wobbly high-top tables.

"You know," Tepi began, "music is like this. Sometimes chaotic, sometimes greasy, sometimes sweet... but in the end, it feeds you."

Emily blinked. "Feeds you? Yes... totally! Fries feed me!"

"I meant music," Tepi laughed.

"Oh. Yes! Of course! Music! Exactly. The... uh... fries of the soul."

Tepi laughed even more, and Emily felt simultaneously humiliated and... happy. *Maybe it's not bad when she laughs. Maybe she's not laughing at me... but because of me.*

She dared to look at Tepi's face. And there they were again: those eyes. Warm, deep, a sparkle inside them, as if tiny stars were pulsing to the beat.

Emily blinked, wanting to look away. But she couldn't. It was as if every note of the music, every beat of the bass, was drawing her deeper into that gaze.

And then it happened. Tepi's eyes began to glow. Not harshly, but like tiny spotlights lighting up to the beat of the music. A gentle pulsing, like heartbeats made of light.

Emily gasped, stumbling a step back, grabbing her cardboard tray of fries as if it were a shield. "Y-your... eyes! They... they... they're glowing!"

Tepi smiled gently. "They always do that when someone listens."

Emily felt her heart hammering against her ribs. *Oh no. Oh no, oh no, oh no. This is magic. This is... dangerous. This is... beautiful.*

She wanted to say something. Anything. But her voice was gone. Only the bass remained, booming like a second voice inside her chest.

Tepi leaned forward slightly, her smile mysterious. "Do you hear it? It's starting."

"W-what?" Emily stammered.

And at that exact moment, the music around them broke open—not louder, not wilder, but... different. Every sound took on a melody; every step the people took became a rhythm. It was as if the music were slowly waking up.

Emily held her fries like a microphone. *Oh no. This is not happening. This cannot be happening. I don't sing. I can't...*

But there was already the first note in her throat, shaky and uncertain.

"What’s happening? This can’t be true.
Just standing here. And seeing you.
Makes my heart flutter.
And my words stutter,"

Emily sang, her eyes wide. In shock, she slapped her hands over her mouth and staggered back.

The people around them paused. A couple in neon headbands turned toward her, grinned—and began clapping along to the beat. In the food truck, the vendor suddenly started drumming on the ketchup bucket.

Emily backed away in panic, stumbled against the table, spun around—and found herself right in the spotlight. An imaginary but glaring spotlight that seemed to beam straight down from the sky.

"I... I can't..." she breathed, but her words were absorbed by the music and transformed into a melody:

"I can’t, this isn’t me.
How can it be
that I am singing?
I was born, only for kissing,
to eat the life of my prey.
But to this day
I haven’t kissed one
but how I missed some-
one to loooooove me.
How can it beeee?"

The words simply poured out of her. Melodious, perfectly in sync with the music around them.

Tepi stepped beside her, calm, self-assured, her eyes still glowing like pulsing stars. She raised her hands—and suddenly, a whole rock orchestra answered her call.

"You are more than your legacy.
It’s not your destiny
to steal the life of your loooove.
So let me give you a shoooove!
Take the reins, don’t dread the deed
you know you have to feed.

You can do it!
Taste the nectar of life!
You can do it!
Don’t succumb to temptation
drink in moderation!"

A chorus of festival-goers accompanied Tepi's refrain. Emily gasped. *She's... singing to me?! How can she sound so beautiful? Like a mix of a butterfly and a rock goddess!*

And before she knew it, she answered, her voice louder this time, driven by the rhythm.

"I don’t see my way
maybe i will stay
always the same.
There is no one to blame
but meeee.

I’m awkward and silly
And shy and clumsy.
A disgrace to my family.
And i will never be
The woman my mother sees.
The girl my father sees."

Emily was out of breath. From her singing and her emotions. She ran to the left to escape but was stopped by a dancing crowd singing: “Nowhere to hide!” She broke for the other side, and the French fry vendor danced into her path: “Nowhere to flee!”

Tepi reached for her hand and pulled her close. Her voice was slow and deep.

"Stop it … little girl. Stop .. your musing.
Don't try to be everybody's darling. No point in using
your parents' view of you as an excuse.
Trying to be what others see has no use.
You are you. And better for it.
And just like that, a flame is lit.
A fire that warms and doesn’t consume.
But wants for souls to commune.
So be who you want, not who you should.
And in the end, all will be good."

Tepi had wrapped her arm around Emily's waist and pulled her close. Her breathing was soft and steady, while Emily's ribcage felt as if it were trying to break away from her body. Her gaze hung once more on those pulsing eyes, which were slowly growing darker. Tiredness returned to Tepi's expression, but she looked happy. "I believe this is the moment where you tear yourself away and flee," she whispered mischievously, loosening her grip.

Emily nodded uncertainly and freed herself. She took two steps back, turned around once more as if she wanted to say something, closed her mouth again, and then bolted after all.
