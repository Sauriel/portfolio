# Date 3 - The Gentleman's Outfitter

Emily stood in front of the dilapidated building, waiting for her date. *I'm probably about to be kidnapped. That's a good excuse for not showing up to a date, right?* she thought, looking around with a hint of hope. But the van with the suspiciously broken headlight drove right past her.

Instead, an expensive car with tinted windows approached and slowly came to a halt beside her. Uncertainly, Emily shifted from one foot to the other as the rear window rolled down and her date smiled at her. "Emily, you came." Oswald's voice was warm, and he seemed almost surprised to see her. "Um... yes! This was the address, right?" she asked, confused.

The car door opened, and Oswald stepped out, as if that were answer enough. He wore a bespoke dark blue suit with a salmon-colored pocket square and a matching tie. *The color coordination of this suit has more stability than my entire apartment,* Emily thought as she caught a flash of matching socks. *And even the SOLES OF HIS SHOES are the same color? But... no one sees those. Well... I saw them. And I'm impressed... but who goes to that much trouble?*

"Have fun, you lovebirds," the driver called out as Oswald quietly closed the door. "Scram, Ed," her date replied with a laugh that could have sliced an ice cream cake. Warm and sharp at the same time.

"Shall we?" Oswald asked, offering her his right arm. Into his left hand, he had conjured a walking cane out of thin air. *I should have listened to Mother. I am so underdressed... that I... that I... no, I'm just underdressed. Damn.* Emily's arm linked with his automatically, robbing her of any chance of an early escape.

Her free hand nervously tugged at her sweater as they entered the building. The front door had long since fallen off its hinges, but Oswald seemed to head purposefully toward one of the back rooms. "Um... so... I don't want to be a party pooper. But... what are we doing here? Do I need to... I don't know... worry about my organs?" Emily asked, trying to make her anxiety sound like a joke. Oswald actually laughed. *That is definitely a nice laugh. Not a threatening one.* "This is the 'Cirque de la criminalité.'" A brief, meaningful pause followed. "An interactive dinner theater," he explained with a smile. *And here I was looking forward to knife-throwing clowns and fire-spitting pickpockets,* Emily thought with relief.

Emily blinked as they entered the dimly lit room. Candelabras made from old handcuffs cast dancing shadows on the walls, and the long tables were draped in red velvet, piled with cutlery as if preparing to feed an entire army. A sign on the wall announced: "**Menu: Murder, Dessert, and Intrigue**."

Emily stood frozen for a moment. *This isn't creepy. No, no, it's just... original. Original is good. Original doesn't equal organ theft.* Her tail lashed nervously against the doorframe.

"Ah, Mr. Tailcup!" a voice boomed across the hall. A broad-shouldered, bald man walked toward them. His white shirt strained over his muscles, and an old scar was visible at his temple. "Didn't expect to see you here."

Oswald smiled calmly. "Salvatore, I thought you were long retired." – "Ah, you know how it is... murder might have a statute of limitations, but boredom doesn't." *No, no, I'm pretty sure murder has no statute of limitations,* Emily thought, as the man let out a throaty laugh and clapped Oswald companionably on the shoulder. Emily flinched, certain Oswald would plunge backward into the table decorations. Instead, he stood firm as a rock, his hand resting casually on his cane.

Emily tried a polite smile. "Uh... hello...?" The man nodded, briefly looking her over, before turning back to Oswald. "Pretty companion. Hope she can stand the sight of blood." – "Um... I prefer hot chocolate," Emily blurted out before she could stop herself. *HOT CHOCOLATE? Why am I talking about hot chocolate when someone mentions blood?*

Oswald actually laughed, a bright, velvety laugh that simultaneously calmed Emily down and made her even more nervous. "She will survive it," he said, as if talking about a play and not a dinner among shady characters.

The man winked and pointed toward the tables. "Take a seat. The show is about to start."

Emily sat down next to Oswald, her heart pounding. From all around her, she heard whispering and occasional laughter. A waiter with noticeably tattooed hands refilled their water—and winked at her as if he had just decided to steal her purse.

"So..." Emily began haltingly, leaning slightly toward Oswald, "these are... actors, right? Please tell me they're actors." Oswald raised an eyebrow, his smile slightly mocking. "Of course, Emily. They are all actors. Method actors. Ex-cons with years of professional experience. The best in their field."

Emily gasped softly and clutched her napkin in panic, as if the thin piece of cloth could protect her from a gang shootout.

Oswald calmly sipped his water. "Don't worry. They're being paid to entertain us tonight. As long as you are with me, nothing will happen to you."

At that exact moment, a gun went off—or something that sounded suspiciously like one—and a woman in a striped dress collapsed dramatically between the tables, a red rose blossom pinned to her chest like blood. "A murder!" someone shouted enthusiastically.

Emily let out a short shriek, reflexively clinging to Oswald's arm and half-burying her face in his suit. He didn't move, only smiled. "That's exactly what I wanted to show you," he whispered.

The plates clattered softly as the appetizer was served. In front of Emily stood a small bowl with something that looked suspiciously like red soup. *Tomato. Please be tomato.* She nudged it cautiously with her spoon, her eyes darting nervously across the tables.

Just then, a cane struck the floor. A man with a massive mustache, a trench coat, and a wide-brimmed hat entered the hall. His French accent was so exaggerated that it was almost believable. "Mesdames et Messieurs! I am Inspecteur Dubois! No one leaves ze room until we have found ze murderer!"

A collective murmur went through the guests. Emily's eyes widened. *Not leave? Not... escape?* Her gaze darted to the door, then to her lucky charm, a tiny plush carrot that she was frantically squeezing in her pocket.

The inspector paced slowly between the tables, scanning the guests with theatrical severity—and finally stopped in front of Emily. "Mademoiselle... you look nervous. Do you have somezing to hide?"

Emily gasped like a startled bird. "I... uh... well... no! Of course not! I... I'm just hungry! Very hungry! I mean... no, not that I'm greedy! But... well, soup! Who doesn't like soup? I mean... murderers, maybe, because... because soup is hot... and... I should probably stop talking, right?"

A few guests giggled. Emily felt her cheeks burning. She stared helplessly at her spoon.

Oswald cleared his throat subtly, his smile as smooth as his suit. "My companion is merely impressed by your magnificent mustache, Inspector. I assure you, she is the most innocent person in the room."

The inspector huffed, seemed to ponder for a moment, and then turned away to question the next guest. Emily exhaled audibly. "Th-thank you," she whispered, staring at her soup as if it were a life raft in a sea of embarrassment.

Oswald leaned toward her, his voice barely audible. "You do know you practically confessed just now, right?" – "I'm just bad at lying!" Emily whispered back in a panic. "If I say I'm innocent, it sounds like I killed someone! And if I say nothing, it does too!"

Oswald's smile deepened, a dangerous, amused glint in his eyes. "Then I am glad I haven't shared my darkest secrets with you yet." Emily stared at him, spoon still in hand. *Was that... a flirt? Or a threat? Or both?*

Silver cloches were lifted from the plates, and a collective "Ooooh" filled the hall. In front of Emily lay something that looked like a tender piece of meat in a red wine sauce. *Or in... blood... no, no, sauce! Sauce, Emily! Stop thinking everything is blood!* She reached nervously for her cutlery, nearly dropping her fork in the process.

"Mesdames et Messieurs!" Inspecteur Dubois struck his cane theatrically once more. "We have come closer to ze murderer. Witnesses report a young woman seen at ze crime scene. Petite, auburn hair, noticeably nervous..." His eyes drifted slowly across the room—and landed, as if by chance, on Emily.

Emily gasped, almost choking on an innocent cube of potato, and threw both hands up in panic. "It wasn't me! I swear! I... I... was at the... uh... the... dessert buffet! I mean, I haven't even seen the dessert yet, but I was thinking about it! And... you can't commit murder while thinking about cake, right?!"

Laughter erupted in the hall. Several guests jokingly shouted, "Guilty!" and clinked their forks against their plates. Emily's tail lashed frantically against the chair legs, her cheeks burning like torches.

Oswald, however, didn't look bothered in the least. He leaned back relaxedly, swirling his glass of wine between his fingers, letting his gaze drift over the crowd. "Interesting how quickly guilt is cast upon an innocent woman," he said calmly, yet his voice cut through the laughter like a knife. "I wonder if that says more about the inspector, or about society."

A murmur rippled through the room. The inspector scoffed with mock indignation. "Are you implying, Monsieur Tailcup, zat I am biased?" – "I'm only saying," Oswald smiled dangerously, "that I know my companion better than you do. And Emily is incapable of committing murder. At worst, she would..." He turned to her, a playful look in his eyes. "...accidentally spill hot chocolate on the killer."

Emily stared at him with her mouth open. "Th-that was an accident!" she blurted out, which only triggered more laughter.

"Aha!" the inspector cried, pointing dramatically at her. "She admits it! She has murdered someone—with cocoa!" The guests roared with laughter, shouting, "Case closed!" and raising their glasses. Emily buried her face behind her napkin, wishing for nothing more than to become invisible.

Oswald, on the other hand, looked highly pleased. He placed his hand calmly on the table, very close to hers—not touching, but close enough for her to feel the warmth. "Don't pay it any mind," he murmured with that dangerous smile. "Today, you're just the prime suspect in a game. Tomorrow, perhaps you'll be... my accomplice."

Emily gasped, her thoughts racing. *Accomplice?! Does that mean... flirting? Or... oh no, or a real accomplice?!*

Dessert was served. Tiny, glossy tarts with chocolate glaze, garnished with red berries. Emily stared at her plate as if it were the first time in her life she had ever seen chocolate. *Okay. Deep breath. I've made it this far. No running away, no window, no accidents. I can do this. Just the dessert left...*

She took a tiny bite, and the sweetness spread through her like a warm crackle. Slowly, the voices in the room began to fade.

Emily imagined herself and Oswald out on the city streets. Not as a nervous couple on a date, but as a *legendary crime duo*. He in his sharp suit, she with her fluttering skirt and oversized sunglasses. Together, they leaped into a souped-up car, cash swirling through the air.

"Come on, Emily, faster!" Oswald cried in her head while she laughed and gripped the steering wheel. Sirens behind them, freedom ahead. They stopped at an abandoned warehouse, dumped the money on the floor, threw themselves into each other's arms, and... Kis... Kissing?! Emily's cheeks burned bright red. In her fantasy, Oswald leaned in close, his smile dark and dangerous, while she nearly fainted from excitement...

"And with zat, mesdames et messieurs, ze case is solved!" The inspector's voice snapped her back to reality. Everyone applauded. Emily's eyes flew open in surprise. *Wait. What? Solved? But... I... I wasn't even paying attention!*

Beside her, Oswald calmly folded his napkin as if he had followed every single second. "You see? No murderer. You survived." – "Y-yes," Emily stammered, still half-lost in her dream world. *And I even survived the kiss... uh... I mean, the dessert.*

The guests stood up; the game was over. With a mixture of pride and panic, Emily realized: she had actually done it. She had made it to the end. Without running away.

But then came the part she had feared the most. Oswald stood up, naturally offering his hand. "Come. I'll take you home." Emily froze. She reached for the plush carrot in her pocket. *Okay, little carrot. Plan B. Maybe I'll just trip. Or say my cactus is sick. Or... or... oh, no. Then he'll definitely want to take me home...*

Oswald waited patiently, his smile as warm as it was dangerous. "Emily?" His voice was calm, and it sounded like an invitation she couldn't refuse. She nodded quickly, way too hard. "Y-yes! Sure! Of course! So... home. Yes. Door. I mean... apartment. So... um... let's go?"

Oswald offered his arm again. Emily linked hers reflexively. Outside, the car was already waiting. The engine purred softly, the tinted windows reflecting the streetlights. Ed sat at the wheel and gave a brief wave.

"Mr. Tailcup, Mrs. Tailcup," he grinned as Oswald opened the car door for Emily and offered his hand. She climbed in as if in slow motion, her knees shaking like pudding.

The door closed with a dull click, and suddenly it was silent. The car rolled forward, smooth as a stalking predator.

Emily didn't even notice that no one had asked where they were going. Instead, her thoughts swirled like a tornado. *Okay... he's taking me home. What... what does that mean? The front door moment. The front door moment means... he might... he definitely wants... Oh no! Should I say something? Or just... run away? No, no, this time I'll hold out. Definitely. Right?*

Oswald sat beside her, elegantly leaning back, his cane held loosely. His gaze rested on her, warm and unfathomable. "You seem thoughtful."

"Me? No! I... uh... never think! I mean... sometimes I do. But not right now!" Emily stammered, squeezing her plush carrot so hard the fabric crinkled.

The smile Oswald gave her was so composed it only made her more nervous.

The streets slipped past, and eventually Emily realized with a shock: they were almost at her apartment. *He knows where I live?! How...? No, no, the car is just driving itself... or Ed knows... or Oswald... or—Oh no. Oh NO.*

Panic coursed through her like an electric shock. Three blocks left. Two. In a moment they would be at her door. In a moment... something was going to happen that she was absolutely not ready for.

"So, Emily..." Oswald began calmly. "It was a..."

He got no further. Because at that exact moment, Emily yanked the door handle, shoved the door open, and threw herself out of the moving car.

Ed cursed, slamming on the brakes. The car came to a screeching halt. But Emily was already back on her feet—running, stumbling, running again, her heart hammering wildly.

*Not home! That's where they're heading! I can't go there! Where then? Anywhere. Just away.*

The cool night air bit her face as she fled into the darkness. Her sweater fluttered, her tail lashing in a panic behind her.

Behind her, the car remained at a standstill with the door open. Oswald didn't even get out. He just sat there, bathed in the glow of the interior light, his smile barely changed, as if he had known all along it would end like this.
