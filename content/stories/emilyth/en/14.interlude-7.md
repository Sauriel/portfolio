# Festival Fever

Emily stumbled into her apartment, her glittery crop top clinging sweatily to her skin, her head pounding as if the festival itself had decided to host an after-show party inside her brain. She kicked off her shoes and barely made it to the sofa, collapsing into the cushions like a stranded seal.

"Oh God... my head..." she muttered. Her wings twitched, and her tail wrapped helplessly around her leg. *Great. I look like a bad mix of a stuffed animal and a disco ball.*

The silence in the room was incredibly loud. Only the refrigerator hummed. Emily rolled onto her side and reached for her phone. *Okay. Call Mom. Mothers know what to do when you're dying. Or at least when you have a fever.*

The ringtone buzzed twice before a familiar voice answered. "Chérie? This late? Are you okay?"

"Maman..." Emily croaked, startled by her own voice. She sounded like a dying bagpipe. "I feel... somehow... sick. I mean, not just sick. Bad-sick. Festival-sick."

Her mother sighed, and even through the phone, Emily could hear her brow furrowing. "I'm sure you haven't eaten anything decent again. Did you at least drink some water?"

Emily blinked at the water bottle sitting on the table, which she had forgotten about. "Uh... well... yes? Fries have oil, oil is liquid, so basically... water?"

"Emilyth!" The tone left no room for argument. "Go make yourself some tea right now, and call me if it gets worse."

"But... I only have cocoa."

"Then drink cocoa. But slowly. And put some socks on. I bet you don't have socks on."

Emily looked down at herself. Barefoot. Of course. "I... wanted to let my toes breathe."

Before her mother could lecture her further, a deeper voice chimed in. "What is wrong with ma petite princesse?"

"Papá!" Emily sat up halfway, only to sink straight back into the cushions. "I feel... urgh. I feel urgh!"

"'Urgh' doesn't sound good," he noted, his voice as warm as a blanket. "Should I come over?"

"N-no!" Emily panicked. "I mean... no, it's just... a little... festival fever. I'm sure it'll go away soon."

"I'm coming," he decided simply, as if the discussion were over. "I'll cook you some soup. Your mother has an excellent recipe."

Emily gasped. "Papá, you can't just..."

"I can," he interrupted gently. "I am going to take care of you."

Her eyes grew wet, all of a sudden. *He says it so easily. So surely. As if it's nothing special. But... it feels like the greatest gift in the world.*

"But... I'm already nineteen," she murmured softly, barely audible. "I should be able to look after myself."

"You are my daughter," he replied. "That never stops. No matter how old you are."

Emily swallowed. Her mother sighed softly in the background. "Do you hear, chérie? Your father is worried. And so am I. We just want you to be well."

"I know..." Her voice trembled. "It's just... I don't want to... always... have to be saved."

"No one is saying that," her father replied. "Sometimes, you're allowed to be weak. Sometimes, you're allowed to be sick. That doesn't mean you are any less strong. It only means you are loved."

Emily pressed the phone tighter to her ear, as if she could feel the warmth of his words through it. Her tail lashed uncontrollably against the back of the sofa. *Damn it. Now I'm crying. Over soup.*

"Papá...?"

"Yes, my little one?"

"Could you... maybe... bring the cocoa? I don't think mine is healing enough."

He laughed deeply. "Deal. Soup and cocoa. I'll be there in an hour."

"And put on some socks!" her mother quickly called out before the line went dead.

Emily set her phone aside, pulled the woolen blanket up over her nose, and closed her eyes. Despite her headache and nausea, there was a little comfort inside her: the certainty that someone was about to arrive who would stay with her. Someone who wouldn't run away. Someone who cared for her.

And while outside, the rain drummed softly against the windows, she whispered into the cushions: "Thank you, Papá."
