# Date 6 - The Inventor

The sun was definitely too bright. At least for Emily's taste. It glared through the museum's high glass roofs, danced on polished floors, and reflected off display cases full of gears lined up right in the entrance area.

Emily stood frozen, clutching her bag to herself. *Okay... deep breath. It's just a museum. A completely normal museum. No one kisses here. Except maybe a statue. Oh no, imagine if I trip and...*

"Emily!"

The voice struck her like an arrow—friendly, lively, full of energy. Miriam approached with a spring in her step, her black curls bouncing in rhythm with her pace. In her simple dress, she looked almost... *fairytale-like. Fairytales with gears? Do those exist?*

"H-hello!" Emily waved her hand awkwardly. It looked like a mix of waving and fending off invisible bees. "So... uh... hello, technology." She grimaced. *Technology?! Why did I say technology?! Oh God, floor, swallow me up, please.*

But Miriam laughed. A warm, bright laugh that simultaneously tickled Emily's stomach and made her knees weak. "You are sweet. Come, I absolutely have to show you something!"

And just like that, she placed her hand on Emily's arm, gentle but firm. Emily's tail twitched nervously under her skirt. *Hand! Hand on my arm! Okay, Emily, stay calm. She's just pulling you along. No reason to go and...*

"This way!" Miriam led her through the hall, weaving through gleaming exhibits. Emily nearly tripped over her own feet, but caught herself at the last second. *Great start. Not. But hey, I haven't run into anything yet. Progress.*

Miriam stopped abruptly—Emily nearly crashed into her back. "Oh! Uh, I didn't mean to... I mean... you are very stoppy." *Stoppy?! Who on earth says that?!*

In front of them stood a small, highly polished steam engine. Copper pipes, a glass container, tiny gears. Everything sparkled in the light like a treasure chest for engineers.

Miriam's eyes shone. "Isn't it beautiful?" Her voice sounded reverent, almost solemn. "You can even operate it! See that lever? It's a miniature model, just like the big machines that used to power entire factories."

Emily blinked. Several times. *Beautiful? I see... pipes. And a hiss. And lots of metal. She talks about it like it's a puppy. But... oh no... her eyes light up so wonderfully when she talks about it.*

"Uh... yes! I mean... it looks... very... steamy." Miriam raised her eyebrows in surprise, then burst out laughing. That bright, infectious laugh that simultaneously made Emily melt and set her heart hammering like a jackhammer.

"Steamy?" Miriam giggled, leaning forward to press a button. The machine hissed to life with a low roar. Hissing, rattling, a small piston puffed out steam.

"WAAH!" Emily threw her arms up as if she'd been attacked by a fire-breathing dragon. Miriam flinched back too—and the two of them looked at each other before bursting into resounding laughter.

Emily clung to the edge of the table, tears welling in her eyes from laughing. *Oh no, I'm laughing too loud. I sound like a dying parrot. But... it's nice. I'm having fun. Real, genuine fun.*

"Well?" Miriam asked, her cheeks rosy, her eyes sparkling. Emily breathed heavily. "So... uh... can you make hot chocolate with it too?"

A moment of silence. Then Miriam laughed even louder, almost enough to make nearby visitors look over in annoyance. Emily was glowing, but something inside her felt proud. *Okay. I made her laugh. On purpose. That is... a win. A real date victory!*

The small steam engine had found its rhythm again, hissing contentedly. Miriam enthusiastically explained something about valves, gear ratios, and pressure. Emily understood... absolutely nothing. But she nodded eagerly, as if she had just defended a doctoral thesis.

*Okay, Emily. Just nod and smile. Nod and smile. She surely won't notice you have no idea... Or she'll notice and find it cute. Or she'll think you're stupid. Or...*

"See, if you turn this, the rhythm changes." Miriam placed her hand on the crank and looked at Emily expectantly. "Um... like this?" Emily grabbed the crank, a bit too hard, and the whole thing gave a loud jolt. A hiss, a crack—both of them jumped back.

"Oh no, I broke it!" Emily gasped. "No, no, it's supposed to do that!" Miriam laughed, reaching over Emily's hands to guide the movement gently. Their fingers brushed briefly. Warm. Firm. Natural.

Emily held her breath. *Hand contact! HAND CONTACT! Stay calm, Emily, or your tail will start lashing and smash some display case to pieces!*

"Perfect." Miriam grinned. "You're a natural." – "Y-yes, totally. A natural at... steam. I'm basically... a boiler." *A boiler?! Why do I say things like that?!*

Miriam laughed, and Emily wanted nothing more than to dissolve into steam herself. But Miriam's smile was so genuine, so warm, that the thought vanished instantly.

They strolled further through the exhibition. Past model locomotives, antique telescopes, and a massive gear clock ticking slowly.

"Tonight... I still have to go to one of those high-society parties." Miriam said it casually, as if it were the most normal thing in the world.

Emily stumbled instantly. "H-high society?! Like... with chandeliers and... champagne and... people who don't eat french fries?" Miriam grinned. "Exactly like that. But honestly, I'd rather be here all day."

Emily blinked over at her. *She just says it like that. As if she weren't... made for those kinds of parties. As if she were like me. Oh no. That makes her even more wonderful. Stop, Emily, stop staring at her like that!*

But then Emily saw it: a tiny shadow crossing Miriam's face, her fingers discreetly rubbing her temple.

"Are you okay?" Emily asked immediately. "I mean... you look as if you're... uh... steaming up inside." Miriam gave a thin smile. "Oh, it's nothing. I've had these headaches for as long as I can remember."

Emily stopped, her heart faltering. *Always? For as long as she can remember? That sounds... bad. So bad. I have to do something.* Her heart gave a strange little leap. Not the sweet, tickly leap she got from Miriam's laughter, but one that felt heavy and important.

Before she could think twice, she placed both hands on Miriam's head. Gently, almost timidly. Her thumbs touched Miriam's temples, as if she could simply stroke the pain away. "Maybe this will help...?" she breathed.

Miriam blinked in surprise but didn't pull back. Instead, she exhaled slowly, a quiet, warm breath brushing Emily's lips.

*Oh no. Oh no, this feels... good. Far too good.*

Emily leaned further forward, resting her forehead against Miriam's. A tingling sensation spread across her skin instantly—first on her hands, then across her forehead, as if someone had scattered sparks. Her heart raced. Her tail lashed uncontrollably under her skirt, but she couldn't look away. Not now.

Miriam's breathing changed, becoming shallower, more restless. Emily felt it: warm, close, dangerously close.

Their mouths found a path, inch by inch. No sudden stumbling, but this slow, inevitable drawing near, like in a dream. Their noses touched, brushing gently against each other, a tiny pressure like an innocent kiss, but with only skin and breath.

*Nose-kiss. Oh God, we just... our noses are kissing! Is that allowed? Is that already...? Oh no... I want more. I want to...*

The warmth of her lips was already there, barely a breath away. Emily could almost feel Miriam's mouth brushing hers. Her heart screamed **YES**, her succubus nature buzzing, pulling, urging...

Every shallow breath from Miriam was warm. A sigh sweeter than any hot chocolate she had ever tasted. Her heart raced, her wings twitching under her sweater as if they wanted to burst open at any second and sweep the museum clean.

*This is crazy. This is wrong. This is... oh no, this is so beautiful.*

Their mouths drew a tiny bit closer. Emily's knees turned soft, her breathing stuttered, her hands held Miriam's head tighter, as if she could stop the world. Their noses rested against each other, rubbing delicately. A tiny pressure, a nose-kiss.

*We are kissing. Well, almost. But basically, yes. Help. Help. I want to. I shouldn't. I...*

Right at the moment she felt the first whisper of Miriam's lips, it burst out of her:

"AAAAH!"

The scream echoed through the entire hall. Emily's eyes flew open; she jumped back, nearly tripping into a display case full of electronics.

"SORRY! SORRY! SORRY!" Her voice cracked as she took off running, past bewildered visitors clutching their museum guides to themselves.

Her bag swung wildly; her tail lashed like a nervous baton under her skirt. Her wings twitched as if trying to break free, but Emily just stumbled through groups of children and past an annoyed security guard.

"I'm so sorry!" she shrieked once more before being catapulted out through the revolving door. *I ruined it again. Again. But... oh no... that was so close. Far too close. I almost...*
