# Date 4 - The Priest

Emily stood in front of the small ice cream parlor as if before a dragon's hoard. The display window was full of colorful scoops, gleaming like jewels in the glow of the neon lights. Strawberry, pistachio, stracciatella, cookies... and chocolate. So much chocolate. Her heart beat faster. But not because of the ice cream. Rather, because of the man who was already sitting outside on one of the small benches.

Damián. Loose shirt, sleeves rolled up, linen trousers that looked as if he had just taken a seaside holiday instead of meeting a young succubus. He seemed completely relaxed, legs crossed, hands folded—and only raised his head when he saw her.

"You actually came." His voice sounded warm and slightly amused. "I was worried I'd have to eat two ice creams all by myself today. A tragic fate, don't you think?" *He's speaking so casually with me. Oh God! Is that okay? Yes, right? I think so! Of course!*

Emily gasped softly and stumbled on her very first step toward him, but managed to stay upright at the last moment. "I-I... yes! I mean... no! I mean... I'm here! And... you are too! Of course! So... yes!" Her tail lashed nervously behind her, and she clutched her bag tighter. *Why do I talk like this? Breathe, Emily. Just breathe.*

Damián grinned and stood up, playfully offering his arm as if he were a gentleman from one of her father's old novels. "Then let us reward your heroism with a scoop of ice cream." Emily turned red all the way to her ears.

Inside, it smelled of vanilla, fresh waffles, and coffee. The server watched them with practiced patience, while Emily stared open-mouthed at the endless display of flavors.

"And for the lady?" the server asked.

Emily pointed frantically first at the strawberry, then the vanilla, then the chocolate. "Uh... that one... or... that one... or... maybe... um... the one with the colorful bits...?" She looked to Damián for help, as if he had theological counsel to offer for this decision.

He laughed softly, ordering three scoops for himself: chocolate, white chocolate, and caramel. And on top: whipped cream, chocolate sauce, and hazelnut brittle. "I don't eat bitter things," he explained with a solemn expression. "Life is bitter enough as it is." Then he turned back to Emily and added playfully, "You should try it too, Emily. Sweetness makes everything easier."

Emily was still staring at the flavors; the server sighed. "So... chocolate," she finally squeaked, "just one scoop! In a... uh... cone! No, in a cup! ... No, a cone after all! I... oh no..."

Damián grinned and took charge of the situation, simply ordering a small cone for her while Emily stood beside him, blushing furiously. When she was handed her ice cream, she tried for a triumphant first bite—and promptly spilled some onto her hand.

"Oh no! I... I'm already..."

Damián leaned in slightly and, with an almost outrageously casual movement, wiped the chocolate from her hand with his finger, then licked it clean with relish. His playful gaze remained locked on her the entire time.

"See?" His smile was mischievous, almost too charming. "Shared already. The first step toward any great relationship."

Emily gasped, nearly choking on her cone, and stammered, "R-r-relationship?! I... well... we... um... ice cream! Yes! An ice cream relationship?!"

Damián laughed softly, took an oversized bite of his chocolate portion, and nodded meaningfully. "Exactly, Emily. An ice cream relationship. The best kind of relationship there is."

The sun hung like a golden disc above the treetops as Emily and Damián slowly strolled through the park. Her cone was dripping dangerously, and a drop of chocolate had already landed on her sweater. Emily instantly pressed her hand against it, as if she could undo the disaster.

*Oh no. A stain! A stain! Does he see the stain? Of course he sees the stain! He sees EVERYTHING! He's probably one of those people who can read your sins right off your forehead...*

"You've got a bit of..." Damián pointed to her sweater, his eyes sparkling mischievously.

"I-I know! It's... fashion! Yes! Stain-fashion! Very modern." Emily held the cone as high as a holy relic, only for the next drop to run down toward her nose.

Damián laughed and handed her a napkin. "You really are an artist of chaos, Emily. If I didn't know better, I'd swear you were doing this on purpose to test me."

Emily gasped and blushed. "T-test? Me? No! I mean... yes? No! I mean... oh God..."

*Why does everything he says sound like a flirt?!*

Damián took a hearty bite of his overloaded cone, chocolate sauce sticking to the corner of his mouth. Instead of wiping it away, he grinned widely. "See? I mess up too. Now we're even."

Emily stared, hypnotized, at the chocolate spot near his lips. *Don't say anything, Emily. Absolutely nothing. Do not say that he looks... cute. No, no, no...* "Cute."

She froze. *Oh no. That was out loud.*

Damián's eyes flashed mischievously. "Ah. So you find me sweet?"

"No! I mean, yes! I mean... I meant the ice cream! The ice cream is sweet! Not you! I mean... you are too, but... no! I mean... yes!" Emily waved the napkin frantically, as if she could sweep the words back out of the air.

Damián laughed deeply—that warm, velvety laugh that instantly turned her stomach into a fairground. "Careful, Emily. You'll get my hopes up like that. And I'm very persistent, you know?"

"Persistent?" She blinked frantically.

"Mhm." He leaned in slightly, his voice deeper but still playful. "I'm like a clingy cat. If you lock me out, I'll just sit on the doorstep until you give up and let me back in."

Emily almost choked on her cone. In a panic, she changed the subject, pointing at a passing pigeon. "Oh! Look! A... uh... bird! Totally pretty! I mean... if you find pigeons pretty... some people do... or..."

Damián looked at her as if she had just made the greatest declaration of love in the world, nodding with exaggerated gravity. "Wonderful. You're right. A pigeon of rare beauty. But it pales beside you, Emily."

Emily stumbled back two steps as her knees suddenly turned to jelly. *He... he is really flirting. With me. On purpose. He... oh no, what do I do now?!*

Their path led them to a small pond where ducks were swimming leisurely in circles. "Oh... ducks!" Emily beamed, instantly leaning forward with enthusiasm. "They're so fluffy. I want to pet them."

Damián raised an eyebrow and stepped beside her, saying nothing but watching her curiously. Emily leaned forward even further—so far that her toes almost lost their grip. A soft "Ah!" escaped her as she pitched forward—and in that same instant, she felt Damián's hand on her arm: firm, warm, completely steady.

"See?" His voice was silky smooth, his grin shameless. "Even the water wants you. I should start getting jealous."

Emily gasped and stumbled back quickly, almost bumping into him. *Hand! His hand! Help, he touched me! And... oh no, that felt nice. No, it is NOT allowed to feel nice!* "I-I just wanted... the... the ducks... uh..."

Damián laughed and shook his head. "Incredible how easily you panic, Emily. I really ought to lead you into temptation more often."

Emily stared at him, her mouth half-open. *Temptation?! Oh no, did he just... that was... a flirt! A real flirt! Temptation! Help, he wants to... oh no, what if he actually... kisses me?! I... I will die. Right now. On the spot.*

They sat down on a small park bench by the water. Above them, the leaves rustled softly; somewhere, a cricket chirped. Emily had long since been holding her cone like a mere prop; the ice cream was nearly finished, but her thoughts were far from sorted.

Damián sat beside her, casual, relaxed, as if he had all the time in the world. While she frantically tried to maintain a straight posture, he leaned back slightly, crossing one leg over the other.

"You see, Emily," he began gently, "you really held out a long time this go-around. Maybe because we started with dessert." His lips twitched with amusement. "I'm almost proud."

Emily laughed nervously, a far too high-pitched giggle. "Yes... uh... well... this time... I'm... uh... being very brave." *Oh no. Brave?! That makes it sound like I'm suffering. It makes him sound like he's evil.*

Then it happened. He placed his hand over hers—so casually that it could almost have seemed accidental. But he didn't pull it back. He just left it there: warm, weightless, a little too long.

Emily froze inside. *Hand! Hand! His hand is on mine! This is... this is... oh no, this feels... nice. Far too nice. Help!*

She raised her eyes to him—and got lost. His eyes were dark, sparkling, almost like stars glowing only for her. She couldn't look away. Couldn't even blink.

*His eyes... why... why is it like... he's pulling me in? Like a magnet. Like a whirlpool. I... I want to...*

Her fingers moved of their own accord. She placed her hand lightly on his arm, feeling the fabric of his shirt, the warmth underneath. And a sudden urge rose within her—raw, unquenchable, like a jolt passing through her. The desire to lean even closer. To touch him. To...

*Kiss him?! Oh God, I want to kiss him! I want to... I HAVE to...*

Emily leaned forward imperceptibly, her heart racing, her wings twitching under her sweater, her tail lashing uncontrollably against the bench. Her lips were just a heartbeat away from...

"No!" she gasped, pulling back in a panic so abrupt that her cone nearly flew out of her hand in a high arc. She leaped up, tripped over the curb, and flailed her arms wildly. "I... uh... I have to... my cactus! Yes! My cactus is... sick! And... and... and my washing machine is running too!"

Damián remained sitting completely calmly on the bench, looking up at her, his smile warm but unmistakably mischievous. "Your cactus... of course." He tilted his head slightly. "But you know, Emily... eventually, you run out of excuses. And then, there is only me."

Emily gasped, blushing all the way down to her neck, and spun around, running off as her heart beat like a snare drum. Behind her, her sweater fluttered, her tail lashed, and she could still hear Damián's voice drifting softly across the park: "See you soon, Emilyth."

She ran, stumbled, ran on—away from him, away from that pull that had nearly swallowed her whole. Away from the kiss she had almost allowed.

*I can't do this... I can't do this! And yet... oh no... I want to.*
