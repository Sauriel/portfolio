# Date 2 - The Police Officer

Nervously, Emily stood before the ticket seller at the medieval festival, holding the two coins she had just bought as admission tickets. She tried to casually juggle them from one hand to the other, like a carefree performer she had seen in the queue ahead of her. It could have looked cool. Could have. Because a moment later, one of the coins slipped from her fingers, hit the ground with a clatter, and rolled in a perfect arc straight against Isabella's shoe.

"Oh no! Sorry, I..." Emily bent down so frantically she almost collided with Isabella's knee. Her fingers swiped at the coin, which naturally leaped away again at that exact moment as if possessing its own survival instinct. Emily caught it and stood back up, her face bright red. "Here. I... uh... saved it."

Isabella raised an eyebrow. Her Victorian pantsuit fit impeccably, her posture upright, every movement precise. *And I thought a leather belt over my wool dress would be enough of a costume.* Isabella smiled slightly. "Very heroic of you."

"That's just... my warm-up routine." Emily pressed the coins into the ticket seller's hand, who watched her with a furrowed brow. *This is going to be a disaster. Or great. Definitely great!*

Together they stepped through the large wooden gate. A bagpipe player was squeezing a song out of his instrument with pure lung power, sounding simultaneously heroic and like a squeaking balloon. Children in ill-fitting knight armor stumbled around, thrashing at each other with plastic swords. From somewhere nearby, the smell of roasted meat and incense wafted over—a mixture that made Emily’s stomach growl and churn at the same time.

Emily dared to steal a cautious glance at Isabella. They had met during her escape from her last date, when Emily crossed the street on a red light. Isabella, in her smart police uniform, had pulled her over and given her a lecture with amiable sternness. Emily had sworn up and down that she would never do it again and that she absolutely could not go to prison. *Far too few escape routes.* And Isabella had only laughed—a beautiful, friendly smile. So Emily had asked her out on a date. And after a brief hesitation, Isabella had agreed and suggested the medieval festival.

Now the police officer walked beside her as if she were part of the royal guard: upright, controlled, hands casually in her pockets. Her Victorian pantsuit made her look as if she weren't here for leisure, but was about to arrest someone for the murder of the spit-roasted pig.

Emily opened her mouth to say something. Anything. About the weather, maybe. Or the bagpipes. Or the little knight-boy who was currently crying and throwing his sword on the ground. But looking at Isabella, she forgot the sentence entirely. Instead, she just smiled awkwardly and nodded.

Isabella nodded back. In silence.

They walked on, past a stall selling "authentic" runestones (Made in China), and past a juggler who was definitely better at it than Emily. She scrambled for words; she had to say something. *Come on, Emily, you can talk. You've learned sentences. Use them!*

"So... um..." she began. Her voice squeaked like a poorly oiled door hinge. "Great place."

Emily was sweating. *Superb. Absolute genius conversation starter. Nobel prize material.*

Isabella nodded. "Yes, I wanted..." She got no further as a street performer bounded into their path, tossing three colorful balls into the air and shouting, "Lovely couple, lovely couple! A kiss for entry!" Emily froze like a deer in headlights, turning bright red and stammering, "We're not... I mean... not yet... this isn't... I... um..."

Isabella locked the performer in a gaze so icy that he nearly dropped his balls and retreated backward into the crowd. Emily exhaled in relief—and promptly tripped over a loose board on the ground.

Flailing her arms, she tried to keep her balance and reflexively grabbed Isabella's sleeve. The officer caught her—firmly, securely, almost elegantly. Emily blinked up at her, lips parted.

"Are you okay?" Isabella asked quietly.

Emily nodded frantically, pulled away, and tried to smooth down her dress as if nothing had happened. "Everything... everything's great. I like tripping. Tradition. It's part of the festival."

Isabella smiled almost imperceptibly. But Emily saw it—a tiny twitch at the corners of her mouth. And suddenly her heart beat twice as fast.

A few steps further, Isabella stopped dead in her tracks. Emily nearly crashed into her back but caught herself just in time, pretending to look intensely interested in a sign.

**Tournament of Blades — Noble Sword Fighting for Everyone!**

A man in chainmail waved a wooden sword theatrically, looking as if he mistook it for a flag.

Isabella's eyes lit up—the first genuine spark Emily had noticed in them. "Sword fighting," she murmured, almost reverently.

Emily blinked. "Oh. Yes. Exactly what I was thinking. Totally... exciting. Blades. Swords. Metal... things."

But Isabella was already a step ahead. She rolled her shoulders, smoothed her pantsuit, and stepped forward. The stall owner welcomed her enthusiastically and handed her a practice sword. "My lady, would you care to show your skill?"

Emily opened her mouth to protest—*My lady?! This is a police officer who can arrest you with eye contact alone!*—but Isabella had already accepted the sword. And suddenly... she changed.

With an elegant sweep, she raised the blade, spun, and let the wood slice through the air—every step precise, fluid, powerful. The crowd held its breath. Emily did, too.

*Wow... she looks like a hero. The kind who saves your life in battle. Or gets the lead role in a movie. Or...* Emily realized she was staring open-mouthed and snapped her jaw shut.

"And you?" The stall owner looked at Emily expectantly. "Would you care to try your hand as well?"

"Me? Uh... no... I... uh... I have to... hold my bag." Emily clutched her satchel with the plush bunny as if her life depended on it.

"Just a little try!" the man urged, pushing a wooden sword into her hands too. Emily stood there, frozen, the sword far too heavy for her thin arms. She swung it once—and the momentum dragged her right along with it. With a wild *woosh!*, she nearly swept the stall's sign away.

"Watch out!" someone yelled. Emily flailed, lost her balance, stumbled, and landed directly in a pile of straw meant for decoration.

The crowd laughed, Isabella lowered her sword, and walked over with an outstretched hand. "Are you alright?"

Emily was half-buried in the straw, her hair full of stalks. "Y-yes. I... uh... just wanted to... test the ground. Very... authentic. Good straw floor."

Isabella offered her hand and pulled her up. Her fingers were strong, firm, but not rough. Emily turned bright red.

"You are... unbelievable," Isabella murmured. Her eyes shone, warm and honest. Emily brushed the straw out of her hair, grinned lopsidedly, and tried not to faint on the spot. Meanwhile, her tail happily kicked up dust as it wagged. "I know. I'm... uh... practicing for the role of court jester."

Isabella laughed softly. A real, gentle laugh. And in Emily's stomach, it felt like an entire army of butterflies wearing chainmail.

They returned their swords and walked a little away from the bustle until they found a relatively quiet bench at the edge of the tournament grounds. A bagpipe player in the distance was practicing a song that sounded like a sack of potatoes crying for help.

They sat in silence for a while. Emily stared at her hands, which were clenched together. *Say something. Ask her something. Anything. Otherwise, she'll think you're some weird straw kid.*

"So..." Isabella began suddenly. Her voice was deeper, calmer, and Emily looked up in surprise. "There is something you should know about me."

Emily nodded immediately, far too eagerly. "Yes! I mean, sure. If you want. Not that I need to know everything. Just... a little. Well... or a lot. Or..."

Isabella raised an eyebrow, and Emily went quiet like a student caught cheating on a test.

"My family... isn't thrilled that I'm a police officer. They had other plans for me. Better ones, as they say." Emily tilted her head to look at her more closely. "Other plans? Like... lady-of-the-castle style? Or... lawyer? Or...?"

Isabella gave a soft huff. "More like 'something presentable.' But this... this is mine. Duty. Order. Justice. I know it sounds old-fashioned, but I need it."

Emily nodded so hard a lock of her hair bounced up and down. "I think that's great! Really! Order is awesome. I... have chaos sometimes. Well, actually all the time. But I... uh... can eat chocolate very neatly. Well, almost."

Isabella's mouth twitched almost imperceptibly, and Emily's butterfly army charged into battle. "And then there's something else," Isabella said more quietly. "My parents have planned an engagement for me."

Emily flinched. *An engagement?! Of course there's someone. Someone like her isn't... free. She's too perfect. Too strong. Too... oh no, stop staring, Emily, stop staring...*

"He is a good man," Isabella continued. "But... I don't know if it's the right thing. I know my family wants it. And maybe part of me wants... normalcy, too." She sighed, her gaze drifting into the distance. "But I wonder if I'm just lying to myself."

Emily opened her mouth, closed it, opened it again. In the end, only a soft "Oh" came out.

"That's why... this." Isabella looked at her, serious, direct. "This is my experiment. I wanted to see what it's like. With a woman. With you. To see if I have to bow to my mother. Or if there is another path."

Emily gasped like a fish out of water. Her thoughts raced. *With me?! So... she wants... to test things?! But I'm... I trip over boards, land in straw, and talk to my hot chocolate! I'm not... test material!*

"I... um... wow... well... um..." Emily struggled for words, her cheeks burning. "I... um... am glad, I think. How... uh... is it going so far?" she asked tentatively.

"Good. Very good," Isabella said, lost in thought. She leaned back, arms loosely crossed, as if she had finished her confession and now just had to see what Emily would do with it.

Emily stared at her, her head full of question marks, exclamation points, and hearts all at once. *Oh no. Oh no, oh no, oh no... this is officially serious.*

They had returned to the crowd when a barker shouted: **"Step right up! Archery! Prove your courage and honor!"**

Emily wanted to steer clear of the stall. But the operator—a widely grinning man with a lopsided hat—pointed directly at her. "Hey, you, the little one there! Give it a try!"

"Me? Oh, no, I... uh... have a dangerous allergy to... bowstrings." Emily laughed nervously. No one laughed with her. Instead, a bow was already being pushed into her hand.

Emily stood there like a cat in the rain. Sweaty fingers gripped the handle; she drew the string. Or at least tried to. The arrow trembled in her hand as if it wanted to escape on its own. With a determined "Uh-hah!" she let go.

The arrow shot forward, missed the target by a hair, and struck the wooden facade of the beer stall next to it with a clatter. The owner, a broad-shouldered man, ducked at the last second. "ARE YOU TRYING TO KILL ME?!" he roared.

"OH GOD, IT WAS JUST AN ACCIDENT! I swear, I'm a pacifist!" Emily shrieked, dropping the bow and raising her hands as if she were about to be arrested.

And then it happened: Isabella laughed. A real laugh. Loud, hearty, so free that for a moment Emily forgot she had just almost committed manslaughter. It was a warm, deep laugh. Totally and absolutely disarming.

Emily stared at her. And then she saw it: a blonde lock of hair had slipped, and peeking out from beneath it were pointed, elf-like ears. Delicate, elegant, not at all matching the strict police officer who normally hid everything about herself so seamlessly.

"Y-your ears..." Emily breathed.

Instantly, Isabella brushed her hair back. "It's nothing."

"Yes! They are... beautiful!" Emily stepped closer, her words stumbling just like her feet. "I mean... fascinating. From a purely visual standpoint. Scientific. I mean... pretty. Not that I... well, pretty, but not... um."

Isabella studied her, puzzled, but there was a tiny spark in her eyes.

But Emily was already long gone. In her mind, she saw herself sitting on a sofa, gently brushing the hair away from Isabella's face, strand by strand. Running a brush through her hair, touching her ears with her finger as if by accident. Tracing the lines of her ears with her fingertips, carefully, tenderly. Very slowly, until Isabella closed her eyes and...

"Emily?" Isabella's voice snapped her back.

Emily's eyes flew open. Her face was burning. "I... uh... well... I... restroom!" she squeaked.

She spun around, immediately tripped over a sign, flailed her arms, and bolted right through the crowd. A street performer almost dropped his juggling balls, a child with cotton candy got bumped aside, and Emily vanished in a chaotic zigzag among the stalls, desperately muttering, "Oh no oh no oh no oh no...!"

Isabella remained behind. Her laughter had faded, but a smile still played on her lips. She watched Emily disappear into the crowd like a startled rabbit, and quietly murmured something...
