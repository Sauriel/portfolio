# Date 8 - The Chicken Soup

Emily lay on her sofa like a plucked pillow that someone had carelessly tossed into a corner. Her blanket was a single tangled knot; her hair looked like an ill-tempered hedgehog. *So this is what dying feels like. Just without the dramatic background music.*

Her head throbbed to the beat of her tail, which snaked beneath the blanket like a musical serpent. "I am officially... dead," she muttered into the sofa cushions. Her plush bunny did not answer. "Well, thanks for the sympathy," she grumbled, pulling the blanket up higher, over her nose.

The doorbell rang.

Emily's eyes flew open. *Papá!* She half-stumbled off the sofa, nearly wrapping herself in the blanket like an oversized spring roll, and struggled toward the door. "I'm coooming..." she wheezed in the voice of a dying opera diva.

She pulled the door open. "Papá..."

Not Papá.

"Uh... hi?" Björn was standing there. With a shopping basket full of vegetables, noodles, bread, and a grin that looked almost offensively healthy.

Emily froze. It took her brain a solid three seconds to process. "Bj-Björn?!"

"Um... yeah." He held up a bag. "Your father couldn't make it. He sent me."

Emily blinked. Several times. *My father... sent the barista... my barista?!* Her horns almost poked through her hair, popping out so quickly.

"You... he... I mean... WHAT?!"

Björn scratched the back of his neck sheepishly. "He said you were sick. And that I'm a good cook. And... well... here I am."

Emily gasped like a fish out of water. *I look like an exploded vacuum cleaner bag. And he's standing here. With vegetables. Help.*

"I... uh... so... do you want to come in?" she stammered, stumbling backward into her apartment. "I mean... not that you have to! But... otherwise you'll just be standing out there. With the vegetables. They'll probably freeze."

Björn just grinned and stepped inside, as if entering Emily's chaos were the most normal thing in the world. His gaze swept over the coffee table piled with a mountain of tissues, over the cushions on the floor, and the blanket she had lost somewhere along the way. "Cozy," he commented dryly.

"It's... uh... vintage chaos. Very trendy. Minimalist," Emily defended herself, hastily kicking a cushion aside as if she could hide the mess that way. Her tail lashed uncontrollably behind her.

Björn set his shopping basket down. "Alright. Let's make some soup then."

"You... want to make it here... in my kitchen...?" Emily's eyes grew wide. "But... the kitchen... it is... uh... occupied. By dust. And crumbs. And... maybe half a piece of toast."

"Perfect. Soup loves the company of toast." He winked and slipped past her into the kitchen as if he were the new head chef in the house of Benoît.

Emily stumbled after him. "I... I'll help you!" she said quickly. "You," he smiled indulgently at her, "are going to rest and just watch." Björn unpacked the vegetables. Celery, carrots, onions, a soup chicken, even fresh parsley. Emily stared at the chicken as if it were an alien artifact.

"That... is... dead."

"That's the whole point of chicken soup."

"Oh." Emily pressed her hand over her mouth. *Don't say anything stupid. Just say nothing.* "Can I... uh... monitor the bread?"

"Highly important." He smiled as he peeled the onions.

An hour later, the apartment smelled of broth and fresh vegetables. Emily sat on a kitchen chair, wrapped in her blanket, watching Björn stir the soup with astonishing calmness. *How does he do that? Like it's magic. Maybe he's secretly a soup wizard.*

"Here." He set a steaming bowl down in front of her, along with a piece of bread. "Medicine."

Emily leaned forward, sniffed it, and felt her heart suddenly grow a little lighter. She took a cautious sip. Warmth spread through her, all the way to her fingertips. "Oh... oh wow... this tastes... like..."

"Soup?"

"...love," she whispered. Then her eyes flew wide. "I mean, not... *your* love! Just... like... generally! Soup is love! Universal!" Her cheeks glowed.

Björn laughed softly. "I'm glad you like my love... uh, soup."

Emily buried her face in the bowl. *Die. Die right now. Or eat more soup. Maybe both.*

When she was finished, Björn tucked her back in on the sofa. "Rest up. I'll be back tomorrow."

"T-tomorrow?!"

"Yes. Morning, noon, and night. I'm taking the shift duty."

Emily stared at him. *He really wants to... come back? Three times? For me?* Her heart stumbled like a nervous rabbit. "Um... okay..."

She felt her eyelids grow heavy. While Björn was still tidying up the kitchen, she drifted off to sleep. The last thing she heard was his soft humming—a melody settling between the steam of the soup and her dreams.

And somewhere deep down inside, she thought: *Maybe... this is the most beautiful date of all.*

The next morning began for Emily with a sound she usually hated: the doorbell. This time it wasn't the shrill shriek of her alarm clock, but a gentle "ding-dong" at the door. She pulled the blanket over her head, muttered something about "zombie apocalypse, please come back later," and hoped the visitor would go away on their own.

There was a knock as well. "Emily? It's me. Björn."

Her eyes snapped open. *Oh God. He's really back. In the morning! Who on earth is functional this early?*

She stumbled to the door, her hair a bird's nest, horns half-visible, pajamas full of creases. "Y-yes?" she breathed, as if she had just sung an opera aria.

Björn grinned. In his hand was a thermos, in the other a small paper bag. "Good morning, patient. Time for your first ration."

"Ration? Sounds... like prison."

"More like a wellness prison." He stepped inside, set the thermos on the table, and grabbed a mug from the kitchen. "Chamomile tea. Good for your throat. And for your fever."

Emily took the mug, her fingers trembling slightly. "Thank you..." She sniffed it cautiously. "Smells like... a liquid flower meadow."

"Exactly." Björn smiled as if that were the greatest compliment. "And now: back to bed."

Emily sank onto the sofa, wrapped the blanket around herself, and watched as he pulled the curtains open a bit. The light almost hurt her eyes, but somehow everything looked friendlier right away. *Why does this feel like... a hospital with an extra-cozy factor?*

"And if you're good and drink it all, I've got a chocolate croissant here for you." He held up the bag. "Freshly baked this morning."

"You baked a chocolate croissant for me?" Emily asked, confused. Björn shrugged. "I'm an early riser. And I know how you sometimes look over at them in the café when I'm baking."

Emily turned red and hastily drank her tea.

At noon, there was another knock. This time Emily was prepared. Well, sort of. She had tried to tame her hair, but the result looked like an exploded cloud. Her lucky charm—today, the little plush frog—sat demonstratively next to yesterday's soup, as if keeping watch.

"Hi," Björn said, placing a bakery bag on the table. "Special lunch menu: soup, and for the second course... a surprise." He conjured a slice of cake from the bag.

Emily gasped. "Cake?! But... I'm sick."

"Being sick doesn't mean being cake-less." He quickly heated up the rest of the soup, placed it on a tray, and sat down next to her. He even blew on the bowl before handing it to her.

Emily blushed crimson. "I... I can do it myself!" She took the soup like a ceremonial artifact and sipped it. The warmth spread, and she couldn't help but sigh. *Okay... this is dangerous. Too delicious. Too close. Too... sweet.*

Meanwhile, Björn opened the cake bag further. "Chocolate cake. Just a small piece. To get your strength back."

Emily tried to look serious. "Well, from a purely medical standpoint... chocolate is a superfood."

"Exactly." He winked. "I've reviewed the research."

They both laughed, and for a moment, Emily forgot her headache. They ate together—her having soup, him a small piece of cake. And then, almost in passing, he talked about his shift at the café: about a customer who sent back her latte art because the heart was "too asymmetrical," about a kid who wanted every hot chocolate to have a different dinosaur in the foam.

Emily laughed so hard she almost swallowed her spoon. "Oh no, you have a difficult life!"

"Yeah. But at least I'm not a dying cosplayer."

"Ha-ha," she mumbled, quickly stuffing cake into her mouth so she wouldn't say anything stupid.

In the evening, he returned. Emily had spent the entire afternoon resolving to look halfway decent. The result: she was now wearing her sweater backward. *Hopefully he won't notice.*

Björn set down another bowl of soup. "Evening program. And... a bonus." He held up a book. "Your literature seminar, right? Want me to read to you?"

Emily stared at him. "You... want to read... to me?"

*How does he know? Did I tell him about it? Yes! But that was months ago. Years! Or did I... talk about it in my delirium? ... Oh God, what else did I tell him?*

"Sure," he replied. "If you're too tired to read it yourself."

She slid deeper into the sofa, using the plush frog as a makeshift pillow under her head. "Okay... but just... a little bit."

Björn sat on the chair beside her, opened the book, and began to read. His voice was warm, not too fast. Every word laid itself over Emily like a blanket.

*Oh no. This is too nice. I can't fall asleep. I have to stay awake. Otherwise... otherwise he'll think...*

Her eyelids grew heavy. Her tail twitched a little more, then went still. She only felt the words, which were like soup for her ears.

Before she knew it, she had fallen asleep.

As she woke up briefly later, she was covered up, the table was cleared, and she could see the freshly washed dishes drying in the kitchen.

The third day began differently. Emily didn't wake up with a feeling of "I'm dying," but rather a feeling of "I still look like death, but internally I might actually live again." Her head wasn't throbbing quite as badly, her nose was halfway clear, and her tail was only lashing at normal speed. *Progress.*

She had barely opened her eyes when the doorbell rang. "Björn," she murmured, almost familiarly now, and dragged herself to the door.

This time he didn't have half a vegetable patch with him, but a large cup. With foam. And—Emily gasped—a little heart in it.

"Good morning," he said calmly. "Medicine, version two. Hot chocolate with a heart."

Emily took the cup like a holy relic. "Oh... you're a... a... lifesaver." She sipped and sighed so loudly that even the plush bunny would have turned red if he could have. "This is... the best medicine."

"Thought so." Björn grinned. "Soup for the body, hot chocolate for the soul."

At noon he returned, a Tupperware container under his arm, soup, and this time fresh baguette too. Emily was much more awake, almost lively. She sat upright at the table without her blanket—a massive victory.

"You look much better already," Björn remarked.

"Do you think so? I made a special effort to stop looking like a picture of misery."

"Mission accomplished."

They laughed, and Emily felt a pleasant warmth spreading through her that had nothing to do with soup. As she ate, Björn talked about the café again—this time about a customer who wanted "vegan cow's milk" and refused to be convinced that such a thing didn't exist. Emily giggled so hard she nearly dropped her spoon.

"You're cute when you laugh," Björn said suddenly, without much emphasis, just like that.

Emily froze, her spoon suspended in midair. *Cute?! Oh no. A compliment! Blushing! Horn alarm!* She quickly lowered her head over the soup as if the broth could make her invisible. "Um... thanks... I mean... you too... I mean... not cute, but... well... sweet. No! Nice! I mean nice!"

Björn only laughed and stirred his soup with a piece of baguette.

In the evening, Emily wanted to return the favor. "Tonight, I'm cooking," she announced death-defiantly. Her sweater was fresh and right-side-out, her hair was halfway tamed, and she had deliberately lined up all her lucky charms on the sideboard.

When Björn walked in, he saw the kitchen—and Emily, staring desperately at a piece of celery as if she could hypnotize it.

"Ah," he said dryly. "The famous look that makes vegetables jump into the pot on their own."

Emily gasped. "It's... complicated! The recipe looked so... simple, but somehow... everything is burning!" A tiny puff of smoke rose from the pan.

Laughing, Björn stepped closer, grabbed the wooden spoon, and started turning down the heat. "Come on, let me help. Otherwise your chicken will end up in the fire department's statistics."

"I wanted to surprise you," Emily murmured meekly, talking to her plush bunny: "I can't even manage celery."

"Hey." Björn gently tapped her forehead. "The surprise is that you wanted to cook at all. That's enough."

Together they stood in the small kitchen, shoulder to shoulder. He showed her how to chop vegetables properly so they wouldn't send pieces flying across the kitchen. Emily held the knife as tightly as if it were a magic wand, trying not to turn completely red every time his hand slid briefly over hers to correct her posture.

"Now, loosen your grip," he said calmly. "You're not a knight in sword combat."

"I feel like one, though." Emily cut a piece of carrot that was far too large. "A very incompetent knight."

"Then I'm your squire."

"But squires don't help knights with... um... chopping, do they?"

"They do today." He grinned, and Emily had to laugh—so hard that tears nearly welled up in her eyes again.

In the end, a soup did make it to the table. Not perfect, but edible. Emily was proud as a peacock with a wooden spoon.

"Voilà!" she cried, setting down the bowl.

Björn raised an eyebrow. "That's French."

"Sure. My family comes from Saint-Cirq-Lapopie. I'm... internationally chaotic."

They ate together. This time, not like patient and caregiver, but like... two people simply sitting at the table together. Emily felt lighter, freer.

Later, as they sank onto the sofa, full and tired, Emily stayed quiet for a moment. Her heart beat, but not in a panic. Differently. Warmly.

Björn sat next to her, his hands loosely in his lap, as relaxed as ever.

"Thank you," she whispered. "For... everything. Soup. Chocolate croissant. Hot chocolate. Reading to me. And... for staying."

Björn looked at her, his smile gentle. "Of course I stayed. Where else would I have been?"

Her cheeks glowed, but she didn't look away. Instead, she pulled the blanket half over herself, half over him. Natural as could be.

Emily leaned tiredly against his shoulder and closed her eyes.

For a moment, there was silence. A silence that wasn't uncomfortable.

Then a phone vibrated in Björn's pocket. Once. Briefly. "Don't you want to check it?" Emily asked sleepily. "No," he replied. "It's just my boyfriend. He wanted to text me when his flight arrives tomorrow."
