# Refuge

The night enveloped Emily in cool air and dark streets. Her sweater fluttered in the wind, her heart pounding like a drum. *Not home! No, no, Oswald is there, or Ed, or both, or maybe... oh God, maybe they're already waiting with a bouquet of flowers AND handcuffs!*

Her steps echoed, stumbling, like an uncoordinated metronome. *Okay, Emily. Deep breath. Everything is going to be fine. Just go for a little walk. And then check carefully to see if they're still there.*

She kept running until her feet burned. Only then did she see where her feet had carried her: an old, brightly lit building. The church doors stood half-open, as if someone had left them unlatched just for her. Emily frozen. *A church? Is that... allowed for succubi? Won't I burn?* She cautiously raised her hand and tapped the doorframe. Nothing. No sparks. No lightning strike. Just wood. *There we go. I am officially harmless. Ha!*

With a cautious creak, she pushed the door wider. Inside, it smelled of candle wax and dust. Pews lined up like soldiers all the way to the altar. And there, bathed in the candlelight, stood a man. Dark hair, serious eyes, and a smile that was dangerously warm.

"Good evening," he said quietly. His voice sounded as if it could simultaneously preach and flirt. "Lost, young lady?"

Emily gasped. "I... uh... well... yes! I mean, no! I mean... I'm just... I... walking! Totally normal! In the night! Like you do! Well... like one does! Not me! But... today I am!" *Oh God, why am I talking like this?!* God didn't answer, but reached for more popcorn.

The man smiled and stepped closer, and Emily recognized the black cassock, the small cross on a chain. *A priest. Oh no. I lied. I'm a sinner. I'm going to be incinerated immediately.*

"You look as if you need someone to talk to." His eyes sparkled—darker, more intense than a priest's eyes really ought to sparkle. "My name is Damián."

Emily blinked frantically. "Oh. Uh. Emily. I mean, Emilyth. But Emily is fine. Or Em. Some people say Emi, but that sounds like... Eminem and... that's... um... never mind."

He laughed softly. It was a warm, velvety laugh that instantly turned her stomach into a roller coaster. "Emily, then. And what brings you to me?"

Emily clutched her bag as if her life depended on it. "I... well... I... can't do dates. I mean... I never last. I always run away! Through restroom windows, through street performers, out of cars..." Her voice cracked. "I am the queen of escape routes. I... I'm... a date failure."

Silence. Her words echoed off the church walls. Emily wanted to hide under one of the pews. *Great. Now he thinks I'm crazy. Which I am. But still.*

But Damián only tilted his head slightly, his lips curving into an almost mischievous smile. "A woman on the run can be very... intriguing. Perhaps you just need the right hunter."

Emily gasped, thinking of her sister. "Intriguing? That was... I mean... unsexy. Extremely unsexy! Nobody finds running away sexy." Her little horns twitched beneath her hair as Damián stepped a bit closer. *Oh no. I am flirting with the priest. This is not good. Are you allowed to do that? He's smiling! Is he allowed to do that? Is that legal? Oh no, I like it. Help.*

"I... um..." Emily stared at the floor, then back up, then down again. Her words stumbled just like her feet. "W-would you... maybe... uh... like to... go on a date with me?"

For a moment, everything was still. Her heart skipped a beat about three times. Maybe four.

Then Damián smiled broadly. "I would love to, Emily. Tomorrow? At ten? In the little ice cream parlor by the city park? They have the best chocolate."

Emily gasped again. "Chocolate?! I... uh... iced cocoa. I'll have iced cocoa. I... uh... oh no... I'm... looking forward to it."

She stumbled backward toward the door, nearly colliding with a pew, and shouted way too loudly, "Alright then! Date! Ice cream parlor! Tomorrow! I'll be going... restroom!" And she was gone.

Damián remained standing alone in the candlelight, hands calmly folded, a mischievous glint in his eyes. "See you tomorrow, Emilyth."
