# Hot Chocolate

The bell above the door chimed as Emily entered Café Amarante. This time she didn't trip. No shaky skirt, no nervous hopping—just a quiet sigh as she breathed in the familiar scent of chocolate, coffee, and fresh waffles. *Finally. No date. No sister. No reason to run. Just... hot chocolate.*

She slipped into her usual spot by the window, placed her bag beside her, and discreetly laid her little lucky charm—today, a red plush frog—on the table. "Guard this, okay?" she murmured, stroking its head.

A moment later, Björn, the barista, set a steaming mug in front of her. "The usual." He grinned widely as she looked down at the foam. This time, there was a heart—with a mustache.

Emily sputtered with laughter, her pitch far too high. "Oh no, it looks like... like a sheriff in love!" Björn winked. "Or like a heart taking part in Movember."

Emily giggled and took her first sip. Instantly, warmth spread through her, from the tip of her tongue to her fingertips. Almost as if she were learning how to breathe again. *Oh, yes. Exactly what I needed.*

"Tell me, Björn..." she began cautiously, spinning the mug between her fingers, "how do you always make this hot chocolate so delicious? Mine at home never tastes so... uh... soul-saving."

Björn leaned down to her as if sharing a secret. "That's easy. I like my job. And when you love what you do, it automatically tastes better."

Emily blinked. "So my hot chocolate is currently..." He laughed. "Exactly. Liquid love. Extra creamy." Emily laughed along, this time without stammering or blushing. "Then you should definitely file for a patent. Saving the world through hot chocolate."

She kept sipping her hot chocolate, and the conversation drifted along quite harmlessly—about favorite recipes, funny customers, the rain outside. The longer they spoke, the more Emily felt her energy returning.

As Emily pushed the empty mug aside and reached for her lucky charm, Björn grinned. "You know, I think it's really cool that you always come by in your cosplay."

Emily's heart skipped a beat. "C-cosplay?" Her hand automatically shot to her hair, where her little horns sometimes peeked through. Her gaze darted to the floor, then back to Björn.

But he just nodded as if it were the most normal thing in the world. "Sure. Horns, wings, the tail... really high-detail. Must take forever to put all that on."

Emily laughed frantically, way too shrill. "Y-yes! Totally! Hours! Every day! Cosplay... exactly..." She pulled her lucky frog closer, as if it could give her an alibi in an emergency.

Björn just shrugged, still friendly as ever. "Respect. I wish my gaming buddies had that much dedication." Then he turned back to the espresso machine.

Emily exhaled slowly. *Okay... don't panic. Everything is fine. He really believes it's just a costume. I am... safe. It doesn't mean anything at all that humans shouldn't actually be able to see these features.* She briefly rested her forehead against the still-warm mug. "Thank you, hot chocolate. You're my best date partner."

Emily stood up, placed a few coins next to her mug, nervously fiddled with her plush frog, and nodded to Björn. "Thanks... well... for the hot chocolate. And for... not laughing."

"Anytime," he grinned, wiping down the counter with a cloth. "Come back when you need a refill." Emily nodded hesitantly and grabbed her bag.

When she opened the door, rain was still drumming against the curb. A cool wind blew against her, making her sweater cling to her as it fluttered. But she smiled, wrapped her arms around herself, and stepped out.

The streets glistened like black mirrors. *Okay... deep breath. I have my strength back. I can handle the next date. Definitely. Or... hopefully. Maybe.*
