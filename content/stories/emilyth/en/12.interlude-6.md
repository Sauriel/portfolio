# Dream or Kiss

The apartment was quiet. Only the low hum of the old refrigerator vibrated through the darkness, while outside, rain drummed against the skylights. Emily sat curled up on her small sofa, her legs tucked under her, a blanket draped over her shoulders. In front of her stood a half-empty mug of hot chocolate that had long since gone cold.

*Fled again.* She pulled the blanket tighter, as if she could cover up the memory with it. Jonas, Isabella, Oswald, Damián, even her professor... every single one of those evenings had escalated somehow. And now, Miriam.

Her wings twitched restlessly under the fabric; her tail wrapped around her leg like a nervous question mark. "This is getting to be too much..." she murmured, more to her plush bunny sitting next to her than to herself.

Vivienne's voice echoed in her head. "Emilyth, chérie, you must learn to use your powers, or they will use you." Her sister had already explained everything to her: the looks, the touches, the energy you drained through a kiss. And the other things. The things Emily preferred not to think about at all.

"Dreams..." She grimaced. Yes, that was the worst part. Succubi could slip into people's dreams. Enchant them so they had... naughty fantasies about them—and then feed off of that. Once anchored deeply enough, you could live off a person's desires for entire nights.

Emily shook her head so hard her hair clip almost fell out. *No. Never. Not me.* Just the thought of it made her cheeks burn. Showing up in someone else's dream, suddenly half-naked, with a look that promised everything... *Oh God, that would be hell.*

"I'm embarrassing enough in reality!" she blurted out. "What would it be like in a dream? I can't even kiss someone... how am I supposed to do... more...? Do I have to explain that I just dropped by by accident?"

The plush bunny didn't answer. He just smiled with his embroidered button eyes, as if it were all completely normal.

Emily pulled her knees closer to her chest. "Vivienne might be able to do that. She looks like a goddess in every dream. But me? I'd probably show up in pajamas." Her voice grew softer. "And then... they would see me. Really see me. Everything."

She didn't want that. No one was going to use her magic that way. She didn't want to be a secret intruder. She wanted... *love*. Real. Honest. Without tricks.

But her stomach growled softly, reminding her that hot chocolate alone wasn't enough. Not for a succubus. Not for long. She could already feel it: the tiredness, the emptiness that haunted her more strongly after every date.

"I have to do something..." she whispered. Her fingers toyed with the rim of the mug. A kiss. Just one. A real one. That would be enough to quiet this emptiness. And yet... every time the moment came, she ran.

Her thoughts hopped from date to date. Jonas's warm laugh. Isabella's beautiful ears. Oswald's dangerous spark that simultaneously frightened and fascinated her. Damián's eyes that had almost consumed her. Moreau's voice, velvety, almost too close. And Miriam's boundless joy over technology.

Emily pressed herself deeper into the blanket. Her heart pounded just from the memory. All of it was lovely. Tempting. But also terribly risky.

*A kiss. Just a kiss.* She repeated the phrase in her mind like a mantra. It sounded simpler. Harmless. Normal. *Humans kiss all the time. Even on TV. Without anyone dying afterward.*

"So..." she muttered, "if I have to choose between dream-espionage and kissing... I'd rather kiss." Her tail twitched in agreement. Or nervously. Hard to tell.

Emily giggled softly, an insecure but genuine chuckle. "Maybe I should write that down. Rule Eleven of the Dating Code: Better to make out than to stumble into someone else's dreams."

She set the cold mug aside and stood up. Her apartment felt even smaller as she paced up and down, the blanket draped like a cape over her shoulders. *Okay. Deep breath. I will kiss on the next date. Definitely.*

She was silent for a few seconds, staring at her bare toes wiggling on the carpet. "Or... maybe... the one after that."

She immediately shook her head. "No! No more dodging. On the next one. Otherwise, I really will end up in some dream. And then..." She made a face. "...then Vivienne will laugh at me for the rest of my life."

Resolutely, she pulled the blanket tighter and nodded to herself. "Alright then. Next time, I stay. I won't flee. No window. No excuse. Just... a little kiss."

Her heart was already beating faster, just at the thought. But amidst the nervousness, there was also something else: a tiny spark of hope. Maybe... just maybe... she could actually do it.

And if not? Well, at least she had the plush bunny. He would never laugh at her.
