# Poulet Provençal

The small apartment smelled of sautéed onions and burnt hope. Emily stood in the middle of the kitchen, holding a piece of paper on which was written in a cursive hand: *Poulet Provençal - Just like Maman’s.*

"*Facile!*" her mother had scribbled next to it. *Easy.* Emily stared at the note, then at the pan, where garlic in oil was currently turning to ash. "Facile, mhm... more like *fatal*," she muttered.

"Is everything alright, ma petite princesse?" a deep voice called from the living room.

Emily startled, frantically sliding the note under the cutting board as if it were a piece of evidence. "Y-yes, Papá! Everything... everything is perfect! Super healthy! Just a little... uh... very... crispy." She blew frantically on the garlic, as if that could somehow restore its color.

"Your mother sent you the recipe?" His voice was warm, familiar.

"Yes... well... yes." She flattened the chicken breast with a wooden spoon as if trying to hypnotize it. "She wrote: *So easy, even Emily can do it.*" Emily made a face. "I think she adjusted it for me."

She heard a chuckle from the other room. "Your mother has always exaggerated. You can do anything you set your mind to."

Emily hastily dumped diced tomatoes into the pan, which promptly splattered more than a bottle of champagne. She gasped, waving her hand, and almost tripped over her tail, which was lashing back and forth like a metronome in her stress. "All good! It's supposed to do that! Haute cuisine!"

"When it's done, we'll eat like kings," her father said, as if that were already decided. Then, after a short pause, more gently: "Emily... you know you don't have to kiss anyone. Not if you don't want to."

Her hand froze over the pan. The wooden spoon dripped tomato sauce onto the meat. "I... I know," she called out way too quickly. *But... don't I have to? Isn't it what I was made for?* Her heart squeezed. "Sometimes it feels like I have to... otherwise... otherwise I'll just stay... hungry."

"You are my little princess," he said quietly, without hesitation. "And you get to decide what fills you up. No one else."

Emily blinked against the stinging in her eyes. She quickly stirred the pan so the sauce wouldn't burn—or so he wouldn't hear her sniffle. "Yes... Papá."

For a while, the only sound was the bubbling from the kitchen. Then he cleared his throat with mock casualness. "And... that barista... what was his name? Björn?"

Emily almost choked on a piece of bell pepper she was nervously chewing on. "B-Björn?! Uh... him... I mean... he only makes hot chocolate! Just... totally normal hot chocolate! With little hearts on it. But... I mean... nothing special. Just... hot chocolate!" Her wings twitched under her sweater. *Oh no. If he only knew that Björn can see my wings...!*

"Hm." His tone was amused, but not demanding. "Hot chocolate, then. I think I should meet him sometime."

"NO!" Emily gasped, almost dropping the wooden spoon. "I mean... uh... no, Papá! I mean... maybe eventually... but... he really is just... a barista. With... milk... and... coffee... and... milk."

Her father laughed softly, warm and deep. "It's alright, ma petite princesse. I'm only teasing."

Emily breathed a sigh of relief, slid the chicken onto two plates, awkwardly garnished them with a basil leaf, and surveyed her work. "Voilà!" she said, picking up the plates. "Healthy. Simple. Chaotic."

"Perfect," her father replied from the other room, clapping his hands.
